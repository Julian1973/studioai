# GATE 3 — THE ANIMATION DOCTRINE
### The Crystal Bears · Enaid Creative · Version of Record
### This document supersedes ALL prior Gate 3, prompt-structure, and emitter instructions. Where any older document, rule note, or session ruling conflicts with this one, THIS document wins. There is one workflow, one prompt shape, one law set. Nothing is added to any of them without a render-proven failure and Julian's explicit ruling (see CHANGE CONTROL).

---

## 0. PURPOSE

Gate 3 turns a Gate-1-signed beat and a Gate-2-signed world into an approved 15-second clip that is unmistakably Crystal Bears: the script's own action, the bible's own characters, the locked look, driven by the real performed voice. The machine executes; Julian judges. Nothing self-advances past his eye.

---

## 1. THE WORKFLOW — EIGHT STEPS, EVERY BEAT, NO VARIATIONS

**STEP 1 — ARM** *(machine, zero cost)*
Preflight runs both manifests (technical + creative) on the beat. Gates 1, 2a, 2b must carry Julian's signature. Every required field present. **A blank is a BLOCK — no fallback text exists anywhere in this pipeline.** Gate ordering is enforced in code: Gate 3 physically cannot arm otherwise.

**STEP 2 — GATHER** *(machine)*
Assets assembled by junction type:
- **Scene opener:** the signed keyframe + cast turnarounds + scene plate. **Nothing inherited, ever** (Scene-Opener Stack Law). No harvest, no @Video1, no re-mint.
- **Relay beat:** the sharpest settle frame harvested from the predecessor take (state reference) + turnarounds + plate. No @Video1 — retired 2026-07-07 (Julian, watching 1.B2's footage: "the video I don't like it either, I think it confuses things"); a still-frame anchor only.
- **@Audio1:** the beat's approved ElevenLabs V3 performance. The voice fires INTO generation and drives it. It is never added in post. A beat with vocal content and no approved V3 track cannot arm.

**STEP 3 — COMPILE** *(the v5 engine — the sole prompt author)*
The prompt is compiled from data. No prompt text is ever hand-authored or hand-patched, by anyone, for any reason. Structure in §2; sources in §3.

**STEP 4 — LINT** *(machine, zero cost — one unified gate)*
The compiled prompt is checked before money moves: word budgets (§2), banned vocabulary, Law 5 (no dialogue words), no appearance prose, no speed adverbs, no negation outside the Negative block, structural congruence with the Reference Prompts (§4), every content line traceable to its cited source. **Fail = the prompt never fires. Fix at data, recompile.**

**STEP 5 — FIRE** *(one render)*
Single seed. Standard tier. The one-render economy: this render is the take.

**STEP 6 — MACHINE VERDICT** *(automatic on landing)*
Clip QA → carryMarks state continuity → spatial adjacency → settle distinctiveness → anti-hold (last-frame extraction). Any red: **one** automatic re-fire. A second red: **hard stop with layer diagnosis** (keyframe / brief / reference / take). Never a third blind roll. Fast tier is authorised for diagnosis probes only, never production takes.

**STEP 7 — JULIAN'S EYE** *(the gate no machine owns)*
The clip lands in Downloads. Julian watches at speed and answers the only questions that matter: does it flow, is it funny, is that the character? **Approval or rejection is recorded as data on the take.** Rejection names ONE correction in one sentence → data fix → recompile → one re-fire. Rejected takes are archived and marked; they never anchor, never source, never resume anything. Resume logic reads approval status — never file existence.

**STEP 8 — HARVEST & ADVANCE** *(machine, approved takes only)*
Sharpest settle frame harvested from the approved take; the next beat arms at Step 1. A scene's final beat instead produces the scene review pack (all clips, in order) and halts. Scene boundary = full reset: new plate, new ambient bed, new generated anchor, relay depth zero. No continuity is owed across a scene boundary.

**Budgets:** 650 words = hard BLOCK. 400 = target. Word count printed on every emit. (Raised 2026-07-07,

**WORD-BUDGET PRESSURE, FLAGGED 2026-07-07 (Julian's decisions 1-4, this same day):** the new anti-hold-safe
relay wording (§4b) alone costs +39 words over the wording it replaces, on EVERY relay beat; a beat's own
`relayOpeningNote`/`spatialAxis` (when authored) and THE DELIVERY LAW's fuller speaker notes (§3) add more on
top. Measured live: 1.B1 (opener, no relay text at all) is 431 words purely from decision 4's scene-look

exceed the 400-word hard BLOCK as currently enforced. Julian's own decision 5 authorized trimming "repeated
technical boilerplate and redundant negatives" to make room — the one genuine instance found and cut was the
duplicate "16:9" between the HEADER and the tech line (above); no other defensible boilerplate/negative
redundancy was found, and none of decisions 1-4's own content was cut to force a fit, since decision 5 also
named physical action, performance direction and spatial continuity as explicitly NOT for removal. This is a
real, structural conflict between decisions 1-4 (the new content) and 5 (the existing cap), not something
this session resolved unilaterally — put to Julian directly ("what do you think?"), who delegated the call.
RESOLVED, same session: `relayOpeningNote` trimmed to drop its own restatement of the lane assignment

not a cut of anything decision 5 protected. The hard BLOCK/target pair raised from 400/250 to 650/400
(`cb_preflight.WORD_BUDGET_BLOCK`/`WORD_BUDGET_TARGET`) — the old numbers predated both the shot-list
restoration (rule 45) and decision 1's own wording, so they were calibrated for a lighter prompt shape than
the one this doctrine now asks every relay beat to carry. 650 gives 1.B2's real 556-word compile headroom
while still catching genuine runaway content on a future beat.

**RULING (Julian, 2026-07-06, live footage review of 1.B1):** the shot list walks the beat's own authored
`cuts[]` one shot per cut — camera framing + that cut's specific action + who speaks — never a flattened
single-sentence summary. The block's earlier 80-word sub-fence is RETIRED by this ruling: watching 1.B1's
actual render, the flattened-summary approach was found to silently drop every cut's camera and named
staging (the zig-zag ladder, the leaf-FWIP-rebound), leaving Seedance nothing concrete to act on beyond one
generic sentence — "where's the action... the cut saying what camera it is, what he's doing... where's the
story beat?" A real per-cut shot list cannot fit multiple cameras and actions in 80 words; the whole-prompt
400-word hard cap was always the real backstop and remains the only one now.

**Standing negatives (every beat, eleven — rule 43/44):** no morphing/redesign/rescale of characters · no extra characters or props · no on-screen text, subtitles, logos or watermarks · no foreign-language speech · no crystals on or attached to the bees · no frozen wings while airborne · no 2D or flat animation · no invented voices · no floating or sinking through ground · no body inflation · no full-body deflation (slumps/dips only). Beat-specific `stagingProhibited` items merge in FIRST, terse — mechanically prefixed "no " if not already a negation (rule 54, 2026-07-08) so every item in the line reads with the same cadence.

## 3. WHERE EVERY LINE COMES FROM — THE FIDELITY LAW

**Nothing is invented that canon already defines. Every content line traces to a cited source or the prompt does not fire.**

| Prompt element | Source of truth | Rule |
|---|---|---|
| Style line | `style.txt` | Verbatim, always |
| Scene look | The scene's own `sceneLook` field (required, `Field(min_length=1)`) | Rule 52 decision 4 introduced it; **Gate-1-native as of rule 53** (`cb_director_schemas.Scene.sceneLook`) — scene-specific atmosphere, reset per scene; distinct from the scene's `look` (plate-composition) field |
| Reference roles | Engine stack logic (opener vs relay) | §1 Step 2 |
| Relay opening note (optional) | The beat's own `relayOpeningNote` field | Rule 52 decision 1 introduced it; **Gate-1-native as of rule 53** (`cb_director_schemas.Beat.relayOpeningNote`) — who starts where, what breaks first; null on most beats |
| Spatial axis (optional) | The beat's own `spatialAxis` field | Rule 52 decision 3 introduced it; **Gate-1-native as of rule 53** (`cb_director_schemas.Beat.spatialAxis`) — fixed lane/blocking law; null on most beats |
| Speaker delivery note | The cut's own `delivery` field (required whenever `dialogue` is non-blank) | Rule 52 decision 2 introduced the emitter behaviour; **Gate-1-native as of rule 53** (`cb_director_schemas.Cut.delivery`, already existed in the schema but was silently discarded by the emitter until decision 2) — acting direction, never the words (Law 6 unchanged) |
| Acting DNA | The show bible character entries (`shows/crystal-bears/bible/`) | Quoted verbatim, cited; movement language only; the bible is the ONLY editor |
| Shot list | The beat's own Gate-1-signed cuts[] — camera framing + action per cut | Script is law; one shot per cut, no flattening; speed adverbs stripped; chaos written as NAMED actions and world reactions (overshoots, ricochets, petals spinning) |
| Vocal placement | The beat's speaker order | Moments placed; words NEVER written (Law 5 — dialogue, hums, Crystal Calls exist only inside @Audio1) |
| Settle | The beat's endState | A living settle: pose held, life continuing — wings, breath, breeze. Never a freeze |
| Camera | The beat's camera data | One primary move; locked during spoken lines (hums/sing-song are motion-exempt); mid-scene beats open on a new angle WITHIN the scene's space (Coverage Law); establishing wides belong to scene openers only |
| Ambience | The scene's locked ambient bed | One line, word-for-word identical across every beat in the scene |
| Negatives | Standing set + beat staging | The only negation in the prompt |

**Never in a prompt:** character appearance (the reference image carries it — names are binding labels welded to references: "Fuzzby is the bee from @图2"), dialogue words, writer-room guidance (dos/donts live at Gate 1 as review criteria), internal production notes, banned vocabulary (it is the crystal cove meadow), "copy exactly"/frame-freeze language (retired canon — "begin on this exact composition" is the form).

**SOURCE CONFIRMED (Julian, 2026-07-06, in direct chat, resolving the row above): "We don't use the Bible. Use the characters in the project. The characters, scenes: there's a show Bible in there as well. You've got all the information locked in the studio."** `shows/crystal-bears/bible/` is not used and is not being populated — `engine/config/characters.json` (each character's own `bible` object: essence, mannerisms, `actingNote`, crystalCall, dos/donts, onPoint) and the existing scene/canon stores (`shows/crystal-bears/canon/*.json`, the beat package's own scene records) ARE the show bible. This is the studio's existing data, already live, already what `cb-studio/app.html`'s character pages render. Acting DNA sources from `characters.json` per `cb_segprompt._v5_acting_dna_source` — settled, not merely defaulted.

## 4. THE REFERENCE PROMPTS — WHAT EVERY COMPILE MUST MATCH

The lint checks every compiled prompt for structural congruence with these. They are documentation of the law, not templates to hand-edit.

### 4a. SCENE OPENER (no predecessor — the four-reference stack)

```
15s, 16:9, 24fps, 3D CGI beat.

[Style law — verbatim from style.txt] [scene's own sceneLook sentence, if authored]

@图1 opening keyframe — begin on this exact composition. @图2 Fuzzby — match exactly.
@图3 Zenny — match exactly. @图4 scene plate — lighting, palette, texture throughout.
@Audio1 — sole source of all vocal sound; animate mouths and full performance to it.

Fuzzby: [his bible physicality, verbatim — crash-bang chaos, mistimed landings, every
disaster played as triumph]. [cited] Zenny: [her bible physicality, verbatim —
deliberate measured grace; the comedy lives in the pause, the slow blink, the deadpan
non-reaction]. [cited]

0–Ns — [shot 1 framing]:
Fuzzby tears through the flower corridor barely in control — overshooting a turn,
ricocheting off a petal that spins behind him, pollen bursting in his wake — singing
his sing-song from @Audio1, while Zenny collects pollen with neat, graceful precision.
Ns–Ms — [shot 2 framing]:
He dives into a flower, overdoes the exit, spins into a leaf — FWIP — the leaf bends
and catapults him off. He tumbles, rights himself, snaps into a proud chest-out pose
and delivers his line from @Audio1 as if every second was the plan. Zenny glides up
and just watches.
Settle:
his pose held but never still, buzzing with pride, wings a blur; her flat, fond stare
beside him.

Negative: [standing eleven + beat staging, terse].
```

Time ranges (`0–Ns`, etc.) are computed, never authored — a mechanical, weight-free division of the
beat's full 15s across however many cuts it has (`cb_segprompt._v5_shot_time_ranges`, rule 54). The
standalone tech-line CLOSER (fps, "smooth cinematic motion, shallow depth of field") is RETIRED — fps
now lives in the HEADER; the prompt ends on the Negative block directly.

### 4b. RELAY BEAT (differs by the @图1 line, plus two optional beat-authored sentences)

```
@图1 is the approved final frame of the previous beat and must be matched exactly as the first
frame only. Preserve character design, scale, expressions, continuity marks, lighting and local
geography. Immediately after the first frame, begin the new action. Do not hold the
previous pose, replay the previous action, reset the characters or introduce unexplained
repositioning. [beat.relayOpeningNote, if authored — who starts where, what breaks first.]
[beat.spatialAxis, if authored — the fixed lane/blocking law for this beat.]
```

@Video1 is RETIRED (Julian, 2026-07-07, watching 1.B2's actual footage: "I don't think we should use
approved... nearby within the same shot... the video I don't like it either, I think it confuses
things... ref 1 which is the final best frame shot, the character references, then the audio"). Two
motion/position signals (a still frame AND a video clip) read as confusing, not clarifying — a relay
beat now opens from the still-frame anchor alone.

The @图1 line's history, same day: FIRST tightened to name the job only ("start from this frame"), no
provenance, after Julian flagged that naming "the final frame of the previous shot" got misread as an
instruction about THIS shot's own ending. SECOND, "open on a fresh camera setup" was dropped as
redundant with the shot list's own per-shot framing (checked against the seedance-prompt-engine.jsx
reference tool's doctrine, which keeps continuity and camera as separate slots). THIRD — SUPERSEDING BOTH
(Julian's ruling, 2026-07-07, decision 1, later the same night): the terser "start from this frame"
sentence is replaced by the wording above. Julian re-added the provenance language ("the approved final
frame of the previous beat") the FIRST pass deliberately dropped — but this time paired with an explicit
qualifier ("matched exactly as the first frame ONLY") and a direct anti-hold counter-instruction ("Do not
hold the previous pose, replay the previous action..."), addressing head-on the exact risk an adversarial
check (three independent agent reads) had just flagged against a similar, unqualified frozen-instant
example Julian had shared for comparison — rather than avoiding the risk by omission, as the first two
passes did. `beat.relayOpeningNote` and `beat.spatialAxis` are OPTIONAL Layer-2 fields, appended when a
beat has one authored (1.B2 is the first) — mechanical, not a new code branch, so any future beat can
carry its own without an emitter change.

THE DELIVERY LAW (decision 2, same day): a cut's speaker note is no longer a bare "{Name} speaks." — when
the cut has an authored `delivery` field, the note becomes "{Name} performs {his/her} vocal beat from
@Audio1 {delivery}." (e.g. "Fuzzby performs his vocal beat from @Audio1 with earnest, hopeful pomp,
presenting the pollen moustache as though it were an official uniform.") — acting direction (tone,
intent, physical performance), never the words. Law 6 stays fully intact and un-reopenable; `delivery`
is quoted the same way Acting DNA is, never paraphrased at compile time.

Everything else compiles identically. CarryMarks (the moustache, the leg pollen, the light) are the hard state gate at Step 6; incidental set dressing is advisory only.

**WORD-BUDGET NOTE (2026-07-07):** this wording is measurably heavier than what it replaces (+39 words on
every relay beat before any beat-specific `relayOpeningNote`/`spatialAxis`/delivery content); see §2's
Budgets note for the measured totals and how the hard cap was raised (400→650) to absorb it.

## 5. CHANGE CONTROL — HOW THIS DOCUMENT STAYS CLEAN

1. **v5 is the sole prompt author.** Any needed change is an emitter or data change, golden-gated, never a hand edit.
2. **No additions without a render-proven failure.** Outside templates, articles, and advice are cross-checked against this document and LOGGED to LAB_BACKLOG — adopted only when a failure on screen proves the gap, and only by Julian's ruling.
3. **The bible is the only editor of character content.** No session, review, or fix ever rewrites DNA — if a character reads wrong, the question is "is the bible being pulled through?", never "rephrase the prompt."
4. **One correction per retake.** Julian names one thing; one variable changes; one re-fire.
5. **This document is the tie-breaker.** Any future instruction that contradicts it is a mistake unless Julian explicitly says "amend the Gate 3 doctrine."

*The emotion is the mechanic. The entertainment is the engine. The bible is the only editor. Julian's eye is the final gate.*
