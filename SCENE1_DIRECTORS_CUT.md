## THE STAGING LAWS (extracted from how Julian directs; the Director cannot soften these)

1. The camera is a character. It chases, dives, climbs and orbits with Fuzzby like a drone, high, low, round and round. It never sits wide and observes. "Wide and warm" openings are banned on comedy beats.
2. Full throttle from frame one. A cold open opens at speed. Energy is the default state; stillness is a spent resource.
3. One gag arc per clip. Setup, impact, recovery, button. Never two arcs in one take. 8 to 10 seconds unless the arc genuinely needs more.
4. The contrast is simultaneous. Zenny works calm, neat and efficient IN the frame while Fuzzby escalates around her. Not alternating coverage; one world, two speeds.
5. Cut to Zenny is the punctuation. Her face is the edit. She gets short cutaway reactions (trying not to laugh; eye roll but smiling; the dry sigh), never long coverage. Her stillness is the joke's frame, not the pace.
6. Fuzzby never acknowledges failure. Every recovery is instant, heroic, "as if nobody has seen him." The comedy is the gap between his self image and what we just watched.
7. Escalation ladder. Each gag tops the last: bounce → moustache → tumble → even more pollen → stuck in the flower. If a beat doesn't raise the chaos, it doesn't exist.
8. Holds only on buttons. The superhero pose, the almost laugh, the thunder stop, the final "Ah, Fuzzby." Maximum one hold per clip, under 1.5 seconds. Nothing else pauses.
9. The one full stop is earned. The thunder is the scene's single dead stop, and it lands BECAUSE laws 1 to 8 never stopped before it.
10. Sound is comic percussion. FWIP, THUMP THUMP THUMP, the humming crescendo, the flower's soft absorb. The score chases Fuzzby too, and ducks for every button.
11. A beat never ends on a leaf hit. Leaves are mid-flight bounces only — a beat, a ricochet, a recovery — never the finisher. The flower is always the finisher: the sustained gag, the payoff, the thing the beat lands on.

## THE FIVE BEATS

### 1.B1 — The chase and the pose (Fuzzby solo speaker) · ~10s

Tall flowers, everything swaying, beautiful. Both bees weave flower to flower collecting pollen, then the camera picks Fuzzby up and CHASES him, drone style, high, low, round and round, as he builds speed. Zenny works calm, precise, neat, efficient in the same world. Fuzzby zigzags wilder, humming louder and louder, "BIZZY-BIZZY-BIZZY," dips low into a flower, scoops pollen, overdoes the exit, spins sideways, hits a leaf, FWIP, bounces back into the air, and, as if nobody has seen him, instantly straightens into a wicked superhero chest out pose: "Nailed it." Zenny glides up beside him and watches for the beat.

SPEAKERS: line 1 (the sing song) the larger bee; line 2 ("Nailed it.") the larger bee. The smaller bee speaks no lines in this take.
HOLD: the superhero pose only, on the button.
FEEL: a rollercoaster that ends in a salute.

### 1.B2 — The moustache and the exchange (two speakers, mapped) · ~10s

Fuzzby zooms off again, humming loudly, and sticks his WHOLE face into a flower. Beat. He pulls back out completely dusted in pollen, wearing a magnificent goatee handlebar moustache. CUT TO Zenny: trying not to laugh. Fuzzby, proud, presenting it: "Do I look official?" CUT TO Zenny, deadpan: "Yes, Fuzzby. Officially nuts."

SPEAKERS: line 1 ("Do I look official?") the larger bee; line 2 ("Yes, Fuzzby. Officially nuts.") the smaller bee. Two speakers, two lines, mapped by index.
HOLD: the moustache reveal (under a second), then her almost laugh on the button.
FEEL: a tickle under the nose that becomes a held giggle.
CANON: the goatee handlebar moustache is a locked gag; enters gag_locks.json as required words for this beat.

### 1.B3 — The tumble (Fuzzby solo, minimal or no lines) · ~9s

Fuzzby gasps, wipes at his face, somehow smears the pollen WORSE, dives for another flower, clips a branch, tumbles end over end, THUMP, THUMP, THUMP, lands upside down inside a blossom, little legs kicking, beat, then POPS back up, undimmed. CUT TO Zenny: eye roll, but she's smiling now.

SPEAKERS: per the locked script (if Fuzzby has an exclamation here it maps to him; otherwise no dialogue, wordless take, no @Audio1).
HOLD: upside down legs kicking, under a second, then the pop.
FEEL: a carnival tumble that refuses to hurt.

### 1.B4 — Even more pollen, then the thunder stop (Fuzzby solo speaker) · ~9s

Fuzzby hums louder, dives into another flower, pops back up somehow EVEN MORE covered in pollen than before; Zenny just watching him, it's amazing to see. Then, a distant RUMBLE of thunder. Fuzzby stops DEAD mid hover, wings still beating: "Whoa. Okay, that sounded dramatic." The scene's one full stop.

SPEAKERS: line 1 the larger bee. The smaller bee speaks no lines in this take.
HOLD: the thunder stop, the longest hold in the scene, and the only one over a second.
FEEL: the carnival catching its breath.

### 1.B5 — The storm line and the stuck exit (two speakers, mapped) · ~10s

CUT TO Zenny: she glances toward the sky; the light has shifted slightly, something feels different, nothing visible yet. Dry, watchful: "A storm's coming." Fuzzby snaps straight back into the pose: "Good thing I work well under pressure," and launches, flies into a flower HIGH UP and gets stuck, legs in the air, spinning. CUT TO Zenny, the scene's button, a small sigh: "Ah, Fuzzby."

SPEAKERS: line 1 the smaller bee; line 2 the larger bee; line 3 ("Ah, Fuzzby.") the smaller bee.
HOLD: the stuck flower with legs kicking under her sigh, the exit button.
FEEL: a tiny cool shiver, answered by a soft comic thump.
