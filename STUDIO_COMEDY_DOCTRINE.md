## 1. THE COMEDY MINDS

- **Chuck Jones** · architect of Looney Tunes timing (Road Runner / Coyote) · **THE LAW THAT MAKES IT LAND.** The held beat before the fall, the look-to-camera, the frame-counts, the rule-of-three. Keeps Avery's wildness legible and character-true. The audience is always one beat ahead of Fuzzby.
- **John Lasseter** · Pixar founding director (Toy Story); already the Gate-3 lead for WEIGHT · **EXAGGERATION AND WEIGHT ARE THE SAME CRAFT.** Squash & stretch is principle #1, but Pixar squash conserves volume and obeys mass, so the bonk lands in your stomach. The named guarantee the cartoon never floats. Also: APPEAL — Fuzzby stays cute mid-pratfall, never grotesque.
- **Pete Docter** · Pixar director (Inside Out / Up); already the Gate-3 heart mind · **THE HEART UNDER THE BANG.** The half-second after the bonk where the eyes go human; the comic-to-tender pivot done as a breath, not a gear-grind. The co-watch conscience: the laugh always has a person inside it.
- **Joe Brumm** · creator of Bluey; already the Gate-3 truth-keeper · **THE MODE DIAL AND THE 4–8 GUARDRAIL.** Proof that BIG physical play and real heart aren't a trade-off. Owns the clean switch between slapstick and still-and-true, and the firewall against adult-snark: if a 4-year-old wouldn't feel safe laughing, it's out.

## 2. THE DOCTRINE — BIG WHEN IT'S FUNNY, SMALL WHEN IT'S TRUE

**The law:** every beat is tagged **BIG (funny)** or **TRUE (heart)** *before* it is staged, and the two are never blended in the same instant. The show lives or dies on the clean gear-change.

**The switch:**
- **Go BIG** when the beat is a gag — exaggerated scale, fast snap timing, full-body commitment, the take, the bonk. Commit 110%. Half-hearted big is the worst outcome. This is the funny Julian is banning the show from neutering.
- **Go SMALL** when the beat is heart — real-kid micro-acting, weighted stillness, the wordless held breath, the Crystal-Call surrender. The dial drops instantly to the existing performance/weight/warm doctrine.
- **Switch on a breath, never a snap.** The transition from BIG to TRUE is one shared exhale: the chaos lands, the mass settles, ONE held beat (a blink, a small breath, the crystal flickering the truth) drops the room into the feeling. The hinge is always *weighted* — you feel the mass come to rest before the tone turns. Snap-cutting modes with no settle gives a 4-year-old whiplash.
- **Ban the mushy middle.** A "medium" beat — neither truly BIG nor truly TRUE — is the silent killer: warm-but-flat comedy and big-but-hollow heart. When in doubt at the boundary, *protect the heart* (you can always add a gag; a trampled ache doesn't come back) — but never let "protect the heart" quietly shrink a funny beat. This is a *direction on big comedy, not a ceiling*.

**The two non-negotiables that survive at full size:**
- **WEIGHT — the bang has mass.** Exaggeration scales the POSE and the TIMING, never the physics. Anticipation → impact → follow-through → secondary lag → settle, volume conserved in every squash and stretch. The instant a gag goes floaty, rubber-limbed, weightless, or frozen-mid-air it stops being funny and becomes cheap AI-slop. The bigger the exaggeration, the *stronger* the weight required. (Inherits the existing Lasseter law — never duplicates or contradicts it.)
- **HEART — the co-watch, never mean or empty-zany.** Every gag passes one test: *a 4-year-old falls about laughing while the parent's chest goes warm — in the same second, for their own reasons.* We laugh WITH, never AT: the butt is the situation or Fuzzby's own lovable over-confidence, never a victim, never humiliation at someone else's hand, never contempt. The chaos is self-authored. If only the kid laughs, it's too empty; if only the parent feels it, it's not funny enough. And the crystal never lies, even at peak silly — it can quietly flicker the NEED his "Nailed it." is covering, in the same frame.

## 3. THE ANATOMY OF A CARTOON GAG
The repeatable four-stroke shape. The animator follows it in order. **The hold is sacred — it is where the laugh is born; never even-pace it away.**

1. **WIND-UP / ANTICIPATION** — *telegraph the promise.* The over-confident flourish: big, slow, decelerating, savouring. Crouch deeper / inhale longer the bigger the coming impact. The wind-up must honestly promise grandeur so the snap can betray it. **No wind-up = no laugh, just a glitch.**
2. **THE EXAGGERATED ACTION** — *commit and smear.* Push the pose to its cartoon extreme through the fast bit (squash to a pancake, stretch to a noodle). Snap HARD into the pose, smear/multiples through the speed. The single fastest frame in the shot lands here, on the accent.
3. **THE BANG** — *the impact, big, with MASS.* Sudden stop, a frame or two of overshoot/compression, a settle. Dust/leaves/a single feather puff. Secondary jiggle on the antennae. It must read as if it HURT (cartoon-hurt — instant and painless, fine the next frame). A weightless contact is the cardinal sin.
4. **THE TAKE / HELD BEAT** — *the delayed realisation; the eyes.* The dead beat of TOTAL stillness (~0.5–1s / 8–12 frames at 12fps) where nobody and nothing moves. Then the delayed take: he's still mid-pride, oblivious → the slow dawn (eyes drift down, antennae droop, the penny drops) → the explosion of realisation. The audience already knew; the comedy is the gap until HE finds out. Pupils shrink-then-pop. Antennae are the expression-amplifiers — they whip and recoil a frame behind the head (overlapping action).
5. **THE SNAP-BACK / BUTTON** — *dignity restored, exit on the upbeat.* Peel off the floor, dust off, re-puff the chest, antennae snap to proud, land the crisp button line ("Nailed it."). Every deformation returns to the locked turnaround — the character is immutable; exaggeration is a temporary state the body passes through, never a redesign. **Cut WITHIN a beat of the button** — running the camera past it deflates the laugh.

**Timing notes:**
- **Contrast of tempo is the engine:** slow makes fast funny. Fast–fast–SLOW. The wind-up decelerates so the snap can shock; the hold re-freezes time so the button ticks out clean.
- **The hold-lengths are law, counted in frames not vibes.** Bonk dead-beat ≈ 8–12 frames; delayed-take hold ≈ a touch longer (the realisation has to travel). **The wordless North-Star nadir is the ONE long hold and it is NOT a comic hold — never play a punch-line inside it.**
- **Silence is a timing tool.** Duck music/SFX to silence over the hold; let the bonk land into a vacuum; drop the button line into clean air; music resumes ON the button, not before. (Hard cue to Composer/Post.)
- **Rule of three, then break it.** Establish the pattern clean twice (flourish-works, flourish-works), betray it on the third (flourish-BONK). A running gag's third callback can be the *smallest* version — a single ear-twitch pays the whole setup because the timing is pre-loaded in the kid's head.
- **Escalate then top it, then CUT.** A chain builds bonk → bigger bonk → topper, each a logical consequence of the first cocky choice (never random). Land the topper, don't milk past the laugh.

## 4. FUZZBY'S COMEDY DNA — THE PROUD BUMBLER

**The engine is one immovable gap:** his **WANT** (be seen as a flawless, important professional) versus his **NEED / actual self** (the messy, warm, lovable little bee who buzzes into leaves). The joke is *never* that he's clumsy — it's that he's clumsy **while performing competence**, and then **denies it with total dignity.** The comedy and the heart are the *same gesture*, structurally warm before a single frame is animated.

**His signature shape — always in three beats, beat three is never skipped:**
1. **THE FLOURISH** — over-confident wind-up. Chest puffed, antennae proud and erect, an unnecessarily grand gesture, a little hover-strut. "Watch this flawless professional." He commits 110%, which is exactly why it's doomed.
2. **THE BUMBLE / BONK** — the body betrays the want. He over-reaches, mistimes, trips on his own confidence. Full Avery scale, full Lasseter weight — he REALLY hits ("BUZZZZZZ-CRASH!", canon), antennae whip forward then snap back, a feather puffs, dust settles, eyes pop.
3. **THE COVER-UP** — instant, dignified reclaim. Peel off floor, dust off, re-puff, snap upright, deadpan "Nailed it." The body says recovered; **one micro-tell** (a wobble, a single droopy antenna, a wing that won't quite fold) says he knows. The held beat after is where Docter's heart leaks in and the co-watch fires.

**Signature moves:** the over-confident hover-strut · the grand professional gesture · the Keaton stone-face cover-up (the bigger the disaster, the stiller the recovery) · the delayed take (he is *always the last to know*) · antennae as his emotional barometer (proud-erect → whip-recoil → one-droop-tell → snap-proud) · wings that give him just enough lift to make the over-reach reach *too far*.

**Counterweight:** **Zenny** — smaller, calm, deadpan, claims nothing so is never demoted; frame-position contrast to Fuzzby. Her stillness makes his chaos read bigger; his bravado makes her calm read funnier. Play them against each other.

**Calibration — how big is Fuzzby-big:** he is "The Laughter Maker," bigger and male — **his physical comedy is the biggest in the cast**, the show's full-throttle cartoon engine. Physical mismatch is his signature: LIGHT-and-fast on the wind-up (small bee), lands with cartoon-HEAVY impact (the funny mismatch). The bigness always orbits the wanting — never chaos-for-chaos. The recurring shape is *confidence → collapse → comic-dignity*, and the laugh is that we see the love under the bravado every single time.

## 5. WORKED EXAMPLE — 1.B1 "BIZZY-BIZZY → bumble → Nailed it."
*Directed fully over-the-top, beat by beat. This is the bar. Comedy-beat tag: **BIG.***

**Setup (status claim):** Fuzzby announces he'll do the BIZZY-BIZZY — his big professional pollen-move — for the room. Status claimed: *high, important, in charge.*

- **[WIND-UP — ~14 frames, decelerating]** Fuzzby plants, puffs the chest to its fullest, antennae snap erect and proud. A grand sweeping professional gesture — wings buzz up to a confident hover, a little hover-strut left-to-right. He savours it: holds the peak proud-pose *a half-beat too long* so we see the hope. Eyes bright, locked on his target. Sound: a rising confident "BIZZY-BIZZY—!" wind-up. *(The promise: watch this flawless professional.)*
- **[ACTION / SMEAR — 2–3 frames, the fastest in the shot]** He launches into the move with too much flourish and over-commits — the wings give him just a hair too much lift, he over-rotates. Full smear/multiples through the arc; body stretches to a noodle on the over-reach. *(The body betrays the want.)*
- **[THE BANG — impact + 2-frame overshoot, then settle]** **BUZZZZZZ-CRASH!** He bonks square into the leaf/post. Sudden hard stop, squash to a near-pancake for 2 frames (volume conserved), then overshoot and settle. A single feather puffs up and drifts. Antennae whip forward past the head, then recoil. Dust/leaf-bits scatter with real arcs. Cartoon-hurt — it reads like it *hit*, but he's structurally fine. **Music/SFX duck to SILENCE on impact — the bonk lands into a vacuum.**
- **[THE HELD BEAT + DELAYED TAKE — ~10 frames dead-still, then the dawn]** Total stillness. Nothing moves. He's still got the proud face on — *it hasn't landed on him yet.* The audience already knows. Then the slow dawn: eyes drift down to the wreckage, one antenna droops, the penny drops — pupils shrink, then POP. **Crystal flickers the NEED for a single frame** (small, scared) under the bravado. *(This is the co-watch frame: the kid sees a funny bee mid-realisation; the parent sees the little guy who can't bear to be seen failing.)*
- **[THE SNAP-BACK / BUTTON — crisp, on the upbeat]** Instant reclaim: he peels upright, brushes a fleck of dust off the chest with Keaton-deadpan composure, antennae snap back to proud, chest re-puffs. Crisp, unbothered: **"Nailed it."** ([proudly] over a buried [gulp] — the voice mind's tell). **ONE micro-tell** survives the mask: a single antenna wobbles, or a wing won't quite fold flat. Music resumes ON the button. **Cut within a beat.**

**Why it's the bar:** every stroke of the four-part clock is explicit and frame-counted; the bang has Lasseter mass (squash-overshoot-settle-feather); the hold is *planted stillness*, not a frozen-mid-air glitch; the gag is engineered backwards from THIS bravado (the over-flourished BIZZY-BIZZY), not generic clumsiness; and the same beat lands a slapstick laugh for the 4-year-old and a quiet ache for the parent (the crystal-flicker co-watch). BIG all the way through, snapping softly into a held human half-second at the dawn.
