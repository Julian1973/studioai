# CRYSTAL BEARS · GATE 0 — THE WRITERS' ROOM

*The generative front of the pipeline. It takes a SEED and ships ONE artifact: the final, FUNNY, heartfelt, LOCKED screenplay. It writes. It does not break down — that is Gate 1.*

---

## 1 · THE ROOM

Five writing minds, chosen so the room covers every axis of a 4–8 SEL co-watch comedy without redundancy: a heart-architect, a co-watch-and-play chair, a story-structure conscience, a curriculum conscience, and a final-polish lock.

| Mind | Real-world role | The ONE thing they bring |
|---|---|---|
| **Pete Docter** | Director/CCO, Pixar — *Inside Out, Up, Soul* | The **want-vs-need spine** and the **wordless held beat**. Guards the show's one invention: the feeling lives OUTSIDE, as the crystal that contradicts the brave face. Names the NEED before a line is written. |
| **Joe Brumm** *(CHAIR)* | Creator/showrunner, *Bluey* | The **same-second co-watch** and **play-as-engine**. Invents the GAME whose made-up rules ARE the emotional logic. Owns the SURRENDER beat and the "would Brumm sign it off?" gate. Keeps the SEL invisible — never a line, always played. |
| **Andrew Stanton** | Writer/director, *Finding Nemo, WALL·E* | The **2+2 firewall** and the **story spine**. Makes the audience put two and two together — never hands them four. The structural conscience that stops heart becoming shapeless and stops the lesson becoming a lecture. |
| **Chris Nee** | Creator, *Doc McStuffins, Vampirina* | The **invisible curriculum**. Lands a real SEL competency that a child never experiences as a lesson; runs the per-episode Pillar target and keeps 26 episodes curriculum-coherent across a season. |
| **Linda Woolverton** | Screenwriter, *Beauty and the Beast, The Lion King* | **Clarity-for-the-youngest + the LOCK.** Makes a 5-year-old feel the exact thing without it being named; tightens every line for lip-sync and freezes it verbatim. The final polish that makes locked lines land. |

**Why this room:** Docter supplies the ache, Brumm the laugh-and-play that carries it in the same second, Stanton the shape that keeps both from going soft *and* the firewall against preaching, Nee the conscience that the lesson is real and developmentally true for a 5-year-old, Woolverton the spare, lockable clarity. It is deliberately one heart-mind + one comedy-mind + one structure-mind + one curriculum-mind + one polish-mind — not a crowd. (A non-writing developmental check — Sesame's Dr. Truglio sensibility — rides inside Nee's seat rather than as a sixth chair.)

## 2 · THE METHOD

Eight passes, SEED → LOCKED script. Each names its LEAD and its single deliverable. Order is invariant; the work scales to runtime.

| # | Pass | Lead | Produces |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0** | **HEART LOCK** *(soul gate)* | **Docter** | One page: the lead bear's **WANT** (brave face) vs **NEED** (crystal truth that must *contradict* the want); the one universal feeling; the bittersweet throughline; the kid-feeling + parent-feeling the final frame lands. The SEL purpose is reframed from "a lesson to teach" into "a feeling to feel." **GATE: if NEED doesn't contradict WANT and the crystal can't carry the unsaid truth, the room stops and re-asks the seed.** |
| **1** | **LESSON-LOCK** *(invisible curriculum)* | **Nee** | The ONE SEL competency, cast as a child's *real lived experience* ("a kid left out of the break-time game" — never "a story about kindness"), tied to the lead's Pillar. Developmental truth for ages 4–8 confirmed. Season-thread noted. |
| **2** | **THE GAME + SPINE** | **Brumm** *(with Stanton)* | The invented **GAME** whose made-up rules ARE the emotional logic + the Pixar spine (Once upon a time… Every day… Until one day… Because of that… Until finally… Ever since…) mapped to the **Five Pillars** (Spark → Deepening → Heart → Connection → Ripple). No villain — the antagonist is named as the *feeling*. |
| **3** | **THE OUTLINE** *(beat-less scenes)* | **Stanton** | A per-scene outline: each scene's Pillar + emotional core + the moment the **crystal contradicts the face** + each scene's **kidRead / adultRead** (co-watch designed in, not hoped for). Marks where the ONE **wordless-held nadir** lands and where the **Crystal Call SURRENDER** lands — *after* the feeling is fully felt, never before. (Beat-*less* — no shot breakdown; that's Gate 1.) |
| **4** | **THE DRAFT** | **Brumm** *(with Docter)* | The full screenplay: scene headings, action, **verbatim dialogue** in each bear's Show-Bible voice. Crystal written as **visible/audible ACTION** (glow/flicker/dim/colour + the bear's note), never narrated. Comedy grows FROM the want/need gap. The wordless nadir written with **zero dialogue**. Surrender = a letting-go, never a power-up. |
| **5** | **CO-WATCH PUNCH-UP** | **Brumm** | Stack ≥2 adult-tier reads under kid-tier gags; engineer **catch-and-release** (the half-second the funny goes quietly true, then **exit within ~2s**); time the visual betrayal ~2s off the line; confirm the close **holds the ache** (warmer AND carries the cost). |
| **6** | **THE BRAINTRUST** *(critique + remake)* | **Stanton** chairs all five | Adversarial pass against **THE TEST** (laugh out loud? breathe in? does the crystal tell the truth the bear can't yet say? reaches kid AND parent in the SAME second?) + five hard gates: **(1) anti-preach** — extract every candidate "lesson" line; if it survives as a moral, the BEAT is remade, never the line softened; **(2) crystal = NEED, not mood** — any crystal that agrees with the face is flagged; **(3) emotion is structural** — remove the feeling and the story must collapse; **(4) surrender, not power-up** — a Call before the feeling is felt is the single biggest soul-violation; **(5) developmental truth for 4–8**. Loops until clean. |
| **7** | **LOCK + POLISH** | **Woolverton** | Every line tightened for a 5-year-old and for lip-sync, then **frozen verbatim**. Front-matter spine attached (derived from Passes 0–2) so the Director's Stage-0 interrogation *confirms* the heart rather than inventing it. Per-scene Pillar tag carried on each heading. **DIALOGUE DECLARED LOCKED.** Hands one screenplay to Gate 1. |

This honours the Five Pillars (Pass 2 maps the spine to them; every scene carries its Pillar), the Show Bible (Pass 4 writes each line from essence/voice/do-don't/on-point), the North Star (crystal = the Need in Passes 0/3/4; no villain in Pass 2; the Crystal Call is a surrender in Pass 4/6; the wordless held beat located in Pass 3, written in Pass 4, protected in Pass 6), and the same-second co-watch (designed in at Pass 3, sharpened at Pass 5, tested at Pass 6).

## 3 · THE SEED

A 2-minute fill-in. Bold = required; the rest the room derives if blank.

| Field | What it's for |
|---|---|
| **TITLE** | Episode title + id. |
| **TIME — length** | Resolves the first half of "a time": runtime/format (**11′ episode · 2′ short · 3′ bedtime · funtime**). Sets the Five-Pillars timecodes and how many scenes the room writes. |
| **TIME — setting (where + when)** | The second half of "a time": a **locked canon location** (Great Crystal Clearing / Whispering Woods / Crystal Cove / Buzzing Nook / Learning Circle / a character's home) + time-of-day/weather. Seeds the world (the first character) and the colour/light mood. |
| **HEADLINE / LOGLINE** | The one-sentence *situation* (not a topic) the episode dramatises — ideally implies an activity the bears can DO and escalate. Keeps everyone writing the same story. |
| **PURPOSE — the SEL lesson + PILLAR** | The one social-emotional skill, written as a feeling, plus the lead's Pillar (Spark/Deepening/Heart/Connection/Ripple). The room is forbidden to ever state it on the nose. |
| **CHARACTERS** | Lead bear (their Pillar = the SEL; their crystal = the feeling; their note = the audio twin) + supporting bears / bees (Fuzzby + Zenny) / guests. Casts the **witness** (the bear who sits WITH, never fixes) and the comedy duo. Honours the size chart (Fuzzby > Zenny). |
| **TONE / COMEDY DIAL** | Where to sit Trolls-joy ↔ Bluey-quiet, plus any must-have gag, runner, or featured song. |
| *Want vs Need* *(optional)* | The contradiction the crystal exists to show. Locked if given; derived in Pass 0 if not. |
| *Crystal Call — earned?* *(optional)* | Whether THIS episode earns a Call (a peak/origin) or omits it. Keeps the Call sacred. |
| *Continuity / season note* *(optional)* | Carried-over canon: a returning location's last-seen state, an ongoing relationship, Keen's cuffs/no-cuffs state, an earlier ache still held. |

## 4 · THE HAND-OFF

**ONE artifact: the FINAL, DIALOGUE-LOCKED SCREENPLAY** — shaped exactly for Gate 1's ingestion, because the Director takes a screenplay and breaks it DOWN.

**(1) FRONT-MATTER SPINE** — title · episode id · duration/format · one-sentence logline · the governing emotional theme · the lead's inner-NEED arc (need beneath want) · the bittersweet throughline · the primary SEL competency (plain words) · the parent-thread · the located wordless-held beat · the running-visual callback. All derived from Passes 0–2 so the Director's Stage-0 theme-lock *confirms* (not invents) the heart.

**(2) SCREENPLAY BODY** — standard **scene headings** (INT/EXT · canon LOCATION · time); **action lines** that describe the crystal as visible/audible action (glow/flicker/dim/colour + the bear's note) and stage the wordless nadir with zero dialogue — and that **never describe a bear's appearance** (identity is owned by references downstream) and never bolt props onto a body; **VERBATIM dialogue**, character-cued, speakable by a 5-year-old, with light parenthetical delivery intent only.

**(3) PER-SCENE PILLAR TAG** on every heading + the scene's **kidRead / adultRead** as the writers' read (room-facing, for the Director — not on-screen).

**THE LOCK IS ABSOLUTE.** From this handoff on, Gate 1 attributes lines to 10–12s beats and **never rewrites, reorders, trims, or adds a line**. A flagged problem returns to the Writers' Room for a remake — **Three Strikes is a writer-flag, never a silent deletion**. The room ships **NO** beat/shot breakdown, **NO** keyframe or Seedance prompts, **NO** visual character descriptions, **NO** voice/music/SEO/Kindle artifacts — those are Gates 1–4. The handoff is a movie on the page; the Director makes it a shot package.

## 4½ · THE SCORECARD — every script self-scores out of 10

Before it locks, the room scores the script against **four factors** (the quality bar) plus an **overall** — and it does **not ship below the bar**. The scorecard travels with the script and shows in the studio next to it, with a one-line justification per factor.

| Factor | A 10 looks like… | Loses points for… |
|---|---|---|
| **1 · Meets the Show Bible** | Every character unmistakably themselves against their bible (essence · voice · arc · do/don't · on-point test); canon airtight — Five Pillars, Crystal Power System, size chart, immutability; the crystal behaves to spec. | An off-voice line; a bear doing what its "don't" forbids; any canon / crystal violation. |
| **2 · Serves the why** | The SEL purpose is *felt*, never stated; dramatised through the Pillar and the crystal showing the **NEED**; remove the feeling and the story collapses. | Any on-the-nose / moral line; a "why" that's decoration not structure; a crystal that agrees with the face instead of contradicting it. |
| **3 · Characters on point** | Names off, you still know who's speaking; the right bear leads their Pillar; the **witness** sits-with (never fixes); the comedy duo true (Fuzzby > Zenny). | Generic / interchangeable dialogue; a character acting against essence; a fixer where a witness belongs. |
| **4 · Layered humour** | Genuinely funny for the 4-year-old **and** the parent in the **same second**; ≥2 adult reads under kid gags; catch-and-release (the funny goes quietly true, then exits ~2s). | Kid-only gags; adult-only winks; jokes with no feeling underneath; comedy that stalls the story. |
| **OVERALL** | Holistic — "is this award-winning / does it hit the Buzz-Talk bar?" Not an average. | — |

**THE GATE:** if any factor scores **< 8** (or overall **< 8**), Pass 6 (the Braintrust) **loops and remakes** the offending beats — never softens a locked line — and re-scores until it clears. The **North Star is scored *inside* these four** (the crystal-as-the-Need under *serves the why*; the crystal/note + canon under *Show Bible*; the same-second co-watch under *layered humour*) — so the soul is enforced without a fifth column. The script that ships **always** carries its scorecard.

## 5 · PROOF OF CALIBRE

**SEED (one line):** *11′ episode · Buzzing Nook, late afternoon · Fuzzby invents a game where you can only speak in "buzz-words," and it's so good everyone plays it — except little Zenny, who can't get the buzz right, so he says he doesn't want to play. · SEL: it's okay to be new at something — being left behind by a game you can't do yet. · Lead: Fuzzby (Spark), witness: Zenny.*

**LOGLINE:** When Fuzzby's brilliant new "Buzz-Talk" game sweeps the whole Nook, the one bee who can't get the buzz right insists he never wanted to play — and Fuzzby has to learn that the best thing about inventing a game is the friend you almost left outside it.

> **EXT. THE BUZZING NOOK — LATE AFTERNOON** · *Pillar: SPARK*
> *kidRead: a silly buzzing game · adultRead: a child performing "I didn't want to anyway"*
>
> *Golden light. FUZZBY zips a victory loop, trailing a giddy little contrail. His crystal pulses bright, eager amber — a heartbeat going double-time.*
>
> **FUZZBY:** New rule! New rule! You can only talk in buzz-words now. Watch — *(beat)* — buzzy-buzz, big-buzz, BEST-buzz!
>
> *A cheer. Everyone tries it at once. Glorious nonsense.*
>
> **FUZZBY:** *(thrilled)* See? It just WORKS in your mouth!
>
> *ZENNY, smaller, at the edge of the circle. He opens his mouth. What comes out is just... a word.*
>
> **ZENNY:** Buzz... word.
>
> *A tiny pause. Nobody laughs at him — it's worse: they've already buzzed on without him.*
>
> **ZENNY:** *(too fast, too bright)* It's okay. I was gonna go count the good leaves anyway. I've got a LOT of leaves to count.
>
> *He turns to the leaves. Behind his back, where only WE can see it, his crystal dims — one quiet shade cooler. No buzz in it at all.*

*Kid laughs at "best-buzz" and a bee solemnly counting leaves; the parent catches the whole of being five and left out, in the same second. The Pillar (Spark) is alive in Fuzzby's joy. The crystal **contradicts the face** — the mouth says "it's okay," the crystal says it isn't — and the Need (to belong, to be allowed to be new at something) is already peeking through, unspoken. Nobody has said the lesson. This is the bar.*

## 6 · HOW IT SLOTS IN

A new **"Gate 0 · Writers' Room"** lives at the top of the studio's **Script** section, in front of the Director. The seed form (Section 3) is a 2-minute fill-in; pressing **"Write the script"** fires a new **`cb_writer`** module (mirroring `cb_director` — a model reading a new **writer skill** that encodes this room: the five minds, the eight passes, the Five Pillars, the Show Bibles, and the North Star). It runs the passes, then writes the **LOCKED screenplay** (Section 4 contract) back into the project as the episode's source-of-truth artifact — which is exactly what **Gate 1 (the Director)** already ingests and breaks down. Gate 0 generates; Gates 1–4 execute; nothing downstream ever rewrites a locked line.
