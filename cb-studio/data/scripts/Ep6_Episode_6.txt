FADE IN:

EXT. BUZZING NOOK – NIGHT                            	1
Moonlight. Silence. The honeycomb hangs from its branch. Glistening. Perfect. Untouched. KEEN is perched in the tree above it. Very still. Like an owl. He has been there for quite some time. Keen is disguised as an owl.  He has made himself owl ears from two sticks. He has a leaf cape. He takes a deep breath.

1	KEEN
(very seriously)
Who? Hoot! Who? Hooty Hoo! 

A long beat. FUZZBY opens one eye from the branch below. Stares up at the tree.

2	FUZZBY
That is not what an owl sounds like.

3	KEEN
(more committed)
Yes, it is. I mean …
 Hoot. Hoot.

4	FUZZBY
Still no.

5	KEEN
(full commitment)
HOOOOOOT.

Fuzzby closes his eye. Keen sees his chance. He spreads his arms. Bends his knees. He is going to leap to the honeycomb. He is about to leap. 

6KEEN 
Fuzzby’s asleep.
 Finally, my chance! 

Just as he is about to take flight. CRACK. The branch snaps beneath him and drops to the ground. Flapping his wings frantically, Keen hold in place floating for a brief second before he drops like a stone. THUD. Flat on the ground. The broken branch lands beside him. He lies there. Looks up at the honeycomb. Still hanging perfectly. Still untouched. The impact wakes Fuzzby. He looks at Keen flat on the ground. He looks at the broken branch. FUZZBY zips down from the tree, hovering just above Keen’s head.

7	KEEN
(from the ground recovering) 
Ugh … the branch broke. 
My landing was... a little off.


8	FUZZBY
(mid-air, giggling) 
I know! That’s why it’s so funny. 
By the way, That was not a landing.

Fuzzby performs a dazzling flip and a dip through the air, then settles onto a soft patch of moss with effortless grace.

9FUZZBY
This is a landing

Keen looks past Fuzzby, up at the honeycomb still hanging perfectly out of reach. So close, but still so far away. 

10KEEN
(GROANING)
SIGH … 

He lets out a long, heavy sigh. 

Fuzzby looks up at the honeycomb, then back down at Keen. 

11Fuzzby
Never. Ever. Keen.

Keen sighs. Gets up. Walks away with dignity. Which is not much. Fuzzby watches him go. Still giggling. Then curls back up. Wings folded. Asleep in seconds.

CUT TO:

EXT. MOONLIT POOL – NIGHT	                            2
A still pool in the heart of the forest. Moonlight on the water. The pool is perfectly still. AIDA kneels at the water’s edge. Her ROSE QUARTZ PENDANT glows softly.

In the pool — an image appears. Luna. Wide awake. Staring at the sky. Surrounded by sleeping bears. Trying everything. Finding nothing. The pool water begins to ripple dissolving the image. 

Aida watches the pool. She places a paw over her heart.

12	AIDA
(softly, to herself)
Even the calmest water
needs time to settle.

She looks up at the sky. Gentle. Patient. She will not rush this.

CUT TO:

EXT. GREAT CLEARING – NIGHT                          	3
The bears are having a campout. Soft blankets. Little lanterns. Stars above. Everyone is settled in their spots. Something exciting is planned for tomorrow morning. A sunrise picnic at the top of Crystal Cove. The highest point. The best view. Nobody wants to miss it.

LUNA moves quietly between the sleeping spots. She is the calm one. This is what she does.  She stops by HOWEY, who is bouncing slightly on his pillow. Tucking in a corner of HOWEY’S blanket. 

13LUNA 
Settling in, Howey?

14HOWEY 
I’m trying! 
But I keep thinking about the sunrise picnic. 
The High Point of Crystal Cove is a big hike!

15LUNA
(tucking in his blanket) 
It is.  That’s why we need a good night’s rest. 
Close your eyes

She moves to MISTY, who is staring up at the canopy of trees with a wide grin. Adjusting MISTY’S pillow.

16MISTY 
This is so fun, Luna. 
We’ve never slept outside before!

	17	LUNA
(adjusting her pillow) 
It’s a special night. 
Sleep well, Misty


LUNA finds FUZZBY already snoring loudly. Half his blanket on the ground. She tucks it back around him.

18AMIE
Night Luna. Sleep well

AMIE smiles warmly and turns off the lantern. 

19LUNA
(gently, to everyone)
Sleep well everyone.
Big day tomorrow.

A chorus of sleepy goodnights. 

Luna settles into her own spot. Pulls her blanket up.
Closes her eyes.

Everyone else is asleep almost immediately. Howey snores softly. Fuzzby twitches his wings dreaming of honey. Zenny breathes slowly and peacefully.

Luna’s eyes open. She stares at the stars.

20	LUNA
(very quietly, to nobody)
Right. Sleeping now.

She closes her eyes tight. She holds them shut for a couple of seconds. Then they pop open again, wider than before. She sits up slightly, looking at the peaceful, sleeping bears around her.

21	LUNA
(to herself)
Hmm. Maybe I’m just... 
not quite ready for bed yet.

She looks up at the vast, twinkling sky.

22LUNA 
I’ll count stars. 
That always helps.

CUT TO:

EXT. GREAT CLEARING – CONTINUOUS	                     4
Luna tries COUNTING STARS. She looks up at the sky. Starts counting. One. Two. Three. She stops. Frowns.

23LUNA
One. Two. Three.
(worried)
I feel bad for three.
Two is a couple.
Four is a family.
Three is just… the awkward middle.
It’s an odd number in a very even world.

She stares at the sky. This is not helping. 

24LUNA 
(to herself) 
Hmm. Counting stars is too... county. 
It’s making my brain feel like an odd number.

She sits up and takes a purposeful, steadying breath.

25LUNA (determined) 
I'll try meditation. 
That always calms me down.

Luna sits cross-legged on her blanket. Very still. Very focused.

26	LUNA
(eyes closed, very calm)
Breathe in sweetness…
breathe out the day…
breathe in the quiet…

27FROG
RIBBIT!

A FROG croaks directly beside her. Luna opens one eye. The frog stares at her.

28FROG
RIBBIT!

29	LUNA
(politely)
Could you possibly… not?

30FROG
RIBBIT.

31	LUNA
(less politely)
Please. 

32FROG
(BEAT/SILENCE)
RIBBIT.

Luna looks at the frog for a long moment. Then very quietly begins to sing. A tiny gentle lullaby. Soft and slow. 

33LUNA
(FROG LULLABY)
Hush little froggy, don’t say a ribbit, Luna’s gonna get you a … uh … (can’t think of the next words/pause) … a lilly pad. It’s so cozy and feels so sweet. It’s gonna put you right to sleep. 

The frog’s eyes grow heavy. It blinks. Once. Twice. It falls asleep. Luna watches it.

34	LUNA
(quietly)
Even the frog can sleep.

She tries again. Closes her eyes. Breathes. In. Out. It’s quieter now. But sleep doesn’t come.

Then she notices something. It’s dark. Really dark. The lanterns have gone out. The forest is full of shadows. Shapes she doesn’t recognise. Sounds she can’t place. She has never slept outside before. She didn’t think about that when she suggested the campout. She is thinking about it now. 

35	LUNA
(very small)
Oh. 
(to herself, whispering)
I never realized... how dark the dark gets. 
I thought I knew the forest.
The forest looks different than it did during the day.

She looks at a gnarled oak tree. In the moonlight, 

36LUNA
Those branches look like fingers … 
reaching out for me! 

She pulls her blanket up to her chin. Her LEPIDOLITE crystal flickers. Getting darker. She makes a small noise. Not quite a word. Just a sound.

A nearby branch creaks. Luna jumps, pulling the blanket over her nose.

37LUNA 
What was that sound? 
It sounded like a... a giant footstep. 
Or a growl.

She looks at the long, stretching shadows on the clearing floor. They seem to move when she isn't looking.

38LUNA 
And that shadow... it wasn't there before. 
Why is it pointing at me?
What does it want!?

She lets out a shaky breath, looking at her sleeping friends.

39LUNA 
I’m the one who suggested the campout. 
It was my big idea. But now... 
I think I’d rather be back in my cozy bed.

Her LEPIDOLITE crystal flickers, pulsing with a dim, nervous lavender light

At that exact moment, a TWIG SNAPS loudly in the bushes behind her. Luna jumps, letting out a sharp, tiny SQUEAK of a gasp. She accidentally kicks her legs out, her foot thumping hard against Sunny’s blanket.
She freezes, clutching her own blanket to her chest, her eyes wide as dinner plates.

40LUNA 
(gasping/SQUEAK) 
Oh! What was—

Sunny stirs. She mumbles, slowly sits up and rubs her eyes.

41SUNNY
(sleepy/waking up)
… marshmallow clouds …

She looks across at Luna. Luna sitting very still and very upset. Blanket pulled up. Eyes wide. Sunny pads over quietly. She sits down beside Luna. Not making a fuss. Just there.

42	SUNNY
(quietly)
Hey.

43	LUNA
I’m sorry. Did I wake you?

44	SUNNY
A little.
Are you okay?

Luna looks at the dark forest around them.

45	LUNA
(honestly)
I’ve never slept outside before.
It’s… darker than I expected.

46	SUNNY
(surprised)
But you’re the calm one.

47	LUNA
(small smile)
Even calm ones get a little scared sometimes.

Sunny looks at the dark forest. Then back at Luna.

48	SUNNY
I know what to do.

CUT TO:

EXT. GREAT CLEARING – CONTINUOUS	                     6
Sunny touches her CITRINE pendant.

49	SUNNY
Bright as the sun,
ready for fun —
Citrine, let happiness run!

Warm golden light blooms from her crystal. It spreads outward gently. Reaching the nearest crystal in the ground. Which glows in response. Then the next. Then the next. Like a trail of tiny lights waking up. Moving through the forest. One by one. The crystals of Crystal Cove lighting up. Softly. Warmly. Like a natural nightlight.

Luna watches. Her eyes wide now — but not with fear. With wonder. She touches her own Lepidolite.

50	LUNA
With quiet and might,
I trust my sight —
Lepidolite, reveal what is right.

Lavender light joins the gold. The two colors weave through the forest together. Fireflies appear from the darkness. One. Then two. Then many. Drifting between the glowing crystals. The whole forest softly lit. Beautiful. Calm. Safe. Luna looks around at the forest she knows. The same trees. The same paths. Just… lit differently.

51	LUNA
(quietly)
It’s the same forest.

52	SUNNY
It’s always been the same forest.

53	LUNA
I just couldn’t see it.

54	SUNNY
(gently)
Even in the dark…
the light is still there.
You just have to let it find you.

Luna looks at Sunny. This is not the Sunny who can’t stop talking about cake. This is a different Sunny. Quiet. Steady. Right here.

55	LUNA
(softly)
Thank you Sunny.

56	SUNNY
(smiling)
That’s what friends do.

They lie back on their blankets. The crystal light glowing softly around them. Fireflies drifting above.
The forest warm and familiar and full of light.

Luna’s Lepidolite glows steady. Her eyes grow heavy. Not because she’s trying. Because she’s finally ready. Her eyes close.

DISSOLVE TO:

EXT. GREAT CLEARING – Morning	                     7

First light. Pale gold through the trees. The crystal lights fade gently as the sun arrives.
The bears begin to stir. Luna is asleep. Really asleep. Peaceful. Still. FUZZBY floats past, still half asleep. Eyes closed. Wings barely moving.

57FUZZBY 
(asleep, mumbling) 
Honey… for breakfast…

Nearby, KEEN is sprawled on his back, his paws moving as if he’s drawing a map in the air.

58KEEN 
(asleep, mumbling) 
Tomorrow… Tomorrow …
best plan ever… it will definitely work…

Fuzzby drifts right over Keen. His wing clips a low-hanging branch, and he tumbles down, landing right on top of Keen's soft belly. Keen doesn't wake up. Instead, he instinctively wraps his arms around Fuzzby like a teddy bear. Fuzzby snuggles into the fur.

59KEEN / FUZZBY 
(in unison, dreaming) 
Mmmm… honeycomb…

The forest is quiet. Everything is exactly as it should be.

FADE OUT.

THE END
