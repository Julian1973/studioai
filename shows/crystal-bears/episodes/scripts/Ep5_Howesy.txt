FADE IN:

EXT. BUZZING NOOK – MORNING	1
Golden morning light filters through the trees.
The honeycomb hangs from its usual branch.
Glistening. Perfect. Untouched. As always.
KEEN crouches behind a bush.
He has a plan. He always has a plan. They never work.
HOWEY stands nearby, shifting nervously from foot to foot.
He is wearing his most casual expression.
It is not very casual.

1	KEEN
(whispering)
Just talk to him. Keep him distracted.
I’ll be in and out in ten seconds.

2	HOWEY
(whispering back)
Talk. Yes. I can talk.

Beat.

3	HOWEY
(still whispering)
What do I talk about?

4	KEEN
(already creeping away)
Anything. Just keep him busy.

Howey walks up to FUZZBY with enormous forced casualness.
Fuzzby brightens immediately.
Someone wants to chat. His absolute favourite thing.

5	FUZZBY
HOWIE! Can I tell you about my breakfast?

Without waiting he launches into a full animated story.
Wings flapping. Arms waving. Full sound effects.

6	FUZZBY (CONT’D)
There was BUZZING! And crumbs!
And a very suspicious berry!

7	HOWEY
A suspicious berry?

8	FUZZBY
Very suspicious Howey.
It was just sitting there.
Looking at me.

9	HOWEY
Berries cannot look at you Fuzzby.

10	FUZZBY
This one could.
It had a look about it.

11	HOWEY
What kind of look?

12	FUZZBY
A berry suspicious LOOK.

Howey stares at him.
Behind them — Keen creeps toward the honeycomb.
Low to the ground. Eyes fixed. Almost there—
Howey sees him. His face fills with guilt.
He is the worst liar in Crystal Cove.
Fuzzby notices. Turns around slowly.
Keen freezes. One foot in the air. Honey cup in hand.

13	FUZZBY
(quietly)
Keen.

14	KEEN
Fuzzby.

15	FUZZBY
Is that my honey?

16	KEEN
It was going to be a gift.

17	FUZZBY
A gift of my own honey.

18	KEEN
Which makes it even more thoughtful.

19	FUZZBY
Never do it again.

Keen sighs. Puts the honey back. Walks away with dignity.
Which is not much.
Howey turns to Fuzzby. Sheepish.

20	HOWEY
I am sorry Fuzzby.
I should not have helped with that.

21	FUZZBY
(waving it off)
It is fine Howey.
I cannot stay angry at you.

Beat. Fuzzby looks at his honey pots. Then at Howey.
The tiniest smile.

22	FUZZBY
Actually… since you’re here…
could you help me fill the honey pots?
There are only about… forty of them.

Howey opens his mouth.
He should say no. He knows he should say no.

23	HOWEY
Uh, uh, well I… (sigh) Of course!

Fuzzby beams. It has begun.
ZENNY drifts past. She shakes her head slowly.

24	ZENNY
You too.

CUT TO:

EXT. CRYSTAL CLEARING – MORNING	2
AIDA sits beside her crystal bowl.
The wand circles the rim slowly.
A low hum fills the air.
Her ROSE QUARTZ PENDANT begins to glow.
Pink light rises across the surface of the bowl.

In the bowl — an image appears.
Howey. Carrying everything. For everyone.
His crystal almost dark. His paws shaking.
His smile never wavering.

Aida watches the bowl.
She places a paw over her heart.

25	AIDA
(softly)
Even the kindest paws
need to rest.

She turns quietly toward the clearing.
She finds ZENNY. Whispers something. Zenny nods.

CUT TO:

EXT. GREAT CLEARING – MORNING	3
Howey is filling honey pots.
He has been filling honey pots for quite some time.
He is cheerful about it. He is always cheerful about it.

SUNNY arrives. She is carrying a very long garland.
She looks at Howey. She looks at the garland.

26	SUNNY
(hopefully)
Howey… you are SO good at hanging things.
Can you help untangle these decorations?

Howey looks at his forty honey pots. Then at the garland.

27	HOWEY
Well, I told Fuzzby I’d fill these honey pots … but … Of course I can help Sunny!

He picks up the garland. Keeps filling honey pots with his other hand.
Sunny beams and skips off.
Howey looks at the garland. Then at the forty honey pots.
Takes a deep breath. Nods confidently. Carries on.

LUNA arrives. She is holding a large mixing bowl.

28	LUNA
(gently)
Howey — I don’t suppose you could…
the cake batter needs stirring and I…

Howey looks at the honey pots. The garland draped over one shoulder.
The mixing bowl now being held out to him.

29	HOWEY
Uh, I’m sorry LUNA. I’ve kinda got a loto n my plate right now ... 

LUNA
It’s your favorite flavor ... BERRY BURST !

HOWEY
OH WOW, Berry Burst?! I guess I can lend a paw. No problem, Luna. I’m happy to help. Leave it with me!

He takes the bowl. Stirs with one paw.
Fills honey pots with the other. Garland still over his shoulder.
Luna smiles gratefully and heads off.
The mixing bowl starts spinning slowly on his arm.
Howey hasn’t noticed. He keeps going.

MISTY arrives. She is carrying a football.

30	MISTY
(quietly)
We need one more for the game Howey.
You’re the only one we haven’t asked.

Howey looks at everything in his arms.
The honey pots. The garland. The mixing bowl.
A football now being held out toward him.

31	HOWEY
I’d love to! But I’m a little busy at the moment.

MISTY
PLEEEEEAAAASE HOWEY … we need one more player. 

He takes the football. Tucks it under his arm.

HOWEY
Well … if you need one more to play then I’m your bear! 

Somehow keeps stirring. Keeps filling. Keeps holding the garland.
Misty smiles and heads off.
The football bounces off his elbow.
He skillfully juggles the ball playing keepy-upy without looking.
He hasn’t even noticed he did it. He keeps going.

AMIE arrives. She has a stack of cushions.

32	AMIE
(kindly)
Howey you are always so helpful…
these cushions just need taking to the oak…

Howey stares at her.
His arms are completely full.
He has a garland around his neck.
Juggling a football with his feet and knees.
A mixing bowl in one paw. Forty honey pots behind him.
He opens his mouth.

33	HOWEY
(giant sigh/through gritted teeth) No problem at all!

Amie places the cushions on top of Howey’s head,
balancing them like a stack of books.
Howey can no longer see over the stack.
Amie smiles warmly and heads off.

KEEN watches from across the clearing.

34	KEEN
(to Amie, quietly)
Has he always been like this?

35	AMIE
Always.

36	KEEN
Does he realize it’s okay to say he can’t help?

37	AMIE
I don’t think he knows how to say no.

Howey passes nearby, attempting to do everything at once.
His paws are shaking slightly.
His Howlite crystal is almost completely dark.
He opens his mouth. Something almost comes out. Almost.

38	HOWEY
KEEN, could you lend me a …
I just need a little …
actually nevermind I’ve got it.

39	KEEN
I CAN  help Howie!

But Howey is already gone.

CUT TO:

EXT. GREAT CLEARING – CONTINUOUS	4
HOWEY is attempting to do everything at once.
He is juggling the football.
The garland is draped around his neck like a very festive scarf.
The mixing bowl is balanced on one arm.
He is carrying Fuzzby’s honey pots.
He looks like a one-man band.
A very large very determined one-man band.
One who absolutely does not need any help.

The pillows fall. He loses control of the football.
It flies up. Howey nods it with his head.
The garland swings forward over his eyes.
The ball bounces off a honey pot. Honey goes everywhere.
He can’t see. He keeps going anyway.
The mixing bowl tips. Cake batter slides slowly toward the edge.

40	HOWEY
(cheerfully, not seeing the disaster)
Everything is absolutely fine!

FUZZBY watches from a safe distance.

41	FUZZBY
(to Zenny)
Should we help?

42	ZENNY
Watch.

The football lands on the mixing bowl.
The mixing bowl launches the cake batter into the air.
The garland catches on a branch and YANKS Howey backwards.
The honey pots go like dominoes. One. Two. Three. All forty.
CRASH. SPLASH. SPLAT.
Howey lands flat on his back in the middle of it all.
Surrounded by honey. Cake batter. Football. Cushions. Garland.
Everything.
A long beat. He lies very still.
One paw slowly gives a thumbs up.

43	HOWEY
(from the ground)
Still fine.

Nobody laughs. The whole group has gathered.
They look at Howey in the middle of the chaos. Then at each other.
Something shifts.

44	SUNNY
(quietly)
We did that.

45	LUNA
We all did that.

A beat.

46	FUZZBY
(very small voice)
Howey down.

Nobody laughs this time.
They all move forward together.
They lift things carefully away. Howey pops up. Still smiling.
Still reaching for the nearest honey pot.

47	AMIE
(firmly, warmly)
No. We shouldn’t have all asked for your help.

Howey stops.
He has never heard Amie say no before. Nobody has.
He looks at her.

HOWEY
NO AMIE, I WANT TO HELP

AMIE
It’s okay to say no, Howie.


48	HOWEY
(a moment of pure, panicked exhaustion)
But I don’t like to say NO.
I like to help.

49	KEEN
You kind of did just say NO… TO AMIE.

50	HOWEY
That doesn’t count.
Oh no, it does count.
Oh no, I said NO!

Howey runs away leaving the pile of stuff behind.

CUT TO:

EXT. STREAM – AFTERNOON	5
A peaceful stream winds through the forest.
Light dances on the water.
HOWEY sits alone at the water’s edge.
His shoulders dropped. His paws open in his lap.
His Howlite crystal glows faintly. Almost dark.

Footsteps on the moss.
AMIE appears. She carries a small honey pot and a flower.
She sits beside him without making a fuss.
Doesn’t say anything straight away. Just sits.

51	AMIE
Zenny asked me to bring you this.

She places the honey pot gently in his paws.
And the flower beside it. Howey stares at them.
He hesitates. Then slowly takes a tiny taste of honey.
Something shifts in his face. Like a knot slowly loosening.

52	AMIE
You give so much to everyone Howey.

53	HOWEY
I just… like to help.

54	AMIE
I know.
But you can’t pour from an empty pot.

Howey looks at the honey pot in his paws. Then up at Amie.

55	HOWEY
If I am not helping…
what am I?

Amie looks at him. No hesitation.

56	AMIE
You are Howey.
That is enough.

A long beat.
Howey stares at her.
Like nobody has ever said that to him before. Maybe nobody has.
Something in his face changes completely.
His eyes glisten. He looks back at the water. Nods slowly.

57	AMIE
(smiling)
Don’t tell Fuzzby.
He’ll want credit for the honey.

Howey laughs. A real laugh. Quiet and gentle. But real.

CUT TO:

EXT. STREAM – CONTINUOUS	6
The stream catches the afternoon light.
Amie touches her Amethyst pendant.

58	AMIE
From within I shine so bright —
Amethyst, let us shine the light.

Soft purple light glows around them. Warm. Steady.
Howey looks at his Howlite crystal.
He places a paw over it gently.

59	HOWEY
Steady and bright,
with peaceful might —
Howlite, guide us right.

White light joins the purple glow. Soft and warm.
The two lights shimmer across the surface of the water.
The stream carries them gently downstream.
Howey watches them go.
He slowly turns his paws upward. Open. Receiving.
His crystal glows warmer now. Fuller.

CUT TO:

EXT. GREAT CLEARING – EVENING	7
The clearing is warm and golden.
The bears sit together in a circle.
They have saved a space. And food. And a soft cushion.
Just for Howey.
HOWEY appears from the treeline.
He stops when he sees it all. He opens his mouth.

60	HOWEY
You did not have to, can I help with …

He pauses. Realises something. Smiles.

61	HOWEY
Thank you.

He sits down. Lets someone else pass him food for once.
He does not jump up to help. He just sits. And receives.
From across the circle Aida watches him quietly.

62	AIDA
(warmly)
Let us give to you today Howey.
That took courage.

Howey looks at her. Nods. That is enough.

FUZZBY appears wearing a flower crown.
It is enormous. Dandelion stems at wild angles.
Petals going in every direction.
He places it proudly on Howey’s head.

63	FUZZBY
(very proud)
My BEST work.

64	ZENNY
Your best work is crashing.

65	FUZZBY
Lopsided crashing.

The crown tilts. Petals fall on Howey’s nose.

66	HOWEY
I can help fix it … (pauses/realizes)
actually, it’s great.
How do I look?

67	KEEN
Magnificent.

Howey beams. Everyone smiles.
The crown tilts further. More petals fall.
Howey reaches up. Straightens it slightly.
Then pauses. Leaves it lopsided. Exactly as it is.
He has learned something today.

ZENNY drifts over quietly.

68	ZENNY
Tomorrow the honey will still be there.

69	FUZZBY
You don’t know that.

70	ZENNY
No.
But I know you will try again.

Fuzzby sighs happily.
Keen puts an arm around his tiny bee shoulders.
The forest is quiet.
Everything is exactly as it should be.

CUT TO:

EXT. BUZZING NOOK – NIGHT	8
Moonlight. Silence.
The honeycomb hangs from its branch.
KEEN creeps toward it.
Very slowly. Very carefully. Almost there—

71	FUZZBY
(appearing from nowhere)
Never. Ever.

Keen freezes.

72	KEEN
I was just—

73	FUZZBY
Never.

74	KEEN
I only wanted—

75	FUZZBY
Ever.

Keen sighs. Walks away with dignity.
Fuzzby watches him go.
Then spots KEEN’S honeycomb sitting on a log.
Unattended. Gleaming.
He looks left. He looks right.
He tiptoes toward it. Almost there—

76	KEEN
(without turning around)
Never. Ever. Fuzzby.

Fuzzby freezes. One foot in the air.
A very long beat.

77	FUZZBY
(genuinely impressed)
How did you DO that.

They sit down side by side beneath the honeycomb.
Neither of them moving. Both of them smiling.

78	FUZZBY
Tomorrow.

79	KEEN
Tomorrow.

The forest is quiet.
Everything is exactly as it should be.

FADE OUT.

THE END
