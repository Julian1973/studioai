FADE IN:

EXT. BUZZING NOOK – MORNING	1
Golden morning light.
The honeycomb hangs from its branch.
Glistening. Perfect. Untouched.
As always.
FUZZBY stands beneath it.
He has a new plan.
He holds a very long vine.
He has tied a leaf to the end of it.
Like a lasso.
He twirls it experimentally.
It wraps around his own leg.
He hops. Untangles it. Tries again.
KEEN watches from nearby.

1	KEEN
(genuinely curious)
What are you doing?

2	FUZZBY
(very focused)
I am lassoing the honeycomb.

3	KEEN
With a leaf.

4	FUZZBY
It is a very strong leaf.

He twirls it again. It wraps around both his legs.
He hops. Falls over. Gets up.

5	KEEN
That is not going well.

6	FUZZBY
(from the ground)
It is going exactly as planned.

Keen watches him for a moment.
Then very casually begins edging toward the honeycomb himself.
One tiny step. Then another.
Fuzzby is busy untangling his legs.
Almost there—

7	FUZZBY
(without looking up)
Never. Ever.

Keen freezes.

8	KEEN
I was helping.

9	FUZZBY
You were not helping.

10	KEEN
I was going to hold it steady. For the lasso.

11	FUZZBY
That is the most creative thing you have ever said.

12	KEEN
Thank you.

13	FUZZBY
I did not mean it as a compliment.

Beat.

14	KEEN
Goodnight Fuzzby.

15	FUZZBY
It is morning Keen.

16	KEEN
I know.

He walks away with what dignity he has left.
Fuzzby watches him go.
Then goes back to his lasso.
He twirls it one more time.
It wraps around a nearby branch.
He pulls.
The branch springs back.
THWACK.
Hits him square in the face.
He sits down.
A long beat.

17	FUZZBY
(to himself)
I am going to need more vine.

AMIE passes by carrying a huge stack of things for everyone.
Misty’s cushion. Sunny’s flowers. Luna’s book. Keen’s water flask.
Howey’s picnic basket. Zenny’s meditation leaf.
Her arms are completely full.
She wobbles slightly under the weight.
She sees Fuzzby sitting with a red nose.
She stops immediately.

18	AMIE
(cheerfully)
Morning Fuzzby!
Are you okay? Do you need any help?

Fuzzby’s vine lasso swings wildly from his paw.
WHOOSH.
It wraps around Amie’s entire stack.
Everything goes flying.
Books. Cushions. Flowers. Flask. Basket. Leaf.
EVERYWHERE.
They both freeze.

19	FUZZBY
(looking at the chaos)
I did not do that.

20	AMIE
(already picking everything up)
It’s okay! Totally fine!
No damage done!

She gathers everything back up.
Restacks it perfectly.
Even the flowers. Even the meditation leaf.
Smiles brightly. Carries on.
Fuzzby watches her go.

21	FUZZBY
(quietly, to himself)
She didn’t have to do that.

ZENNY drifts past.

22	ZENNY
She never doesn’t.

Fuzzby looks at Zenny.
Then at the space where Amie was.
He thinks about this for a long time.

23	FUZZBY
(quietly)
Has anyone ever done that for her?

24	ZENNY
That is a very good question Fuzzby.

Fuzzby looks at his lasso. Puts it down. Thinks.

CUT TO:

EXT. CRYSTAL CLEARING – MORNING	2
AIDA sits beside her crystal bowl.
The wand circles the rim slowly.
A low hum fills the air.
Her ROSE QUARTZ PENDANT begins to glow.
Pink light rises across the surface of the bowl.

In the bowl — an image appears.
Amie.
Always in the background.
Always carrying something for someone.
Always smiling. Always saying yes.
But her Amethyst crystal…
Flickering. Dimming.
Like a candle in a draft.

Aida watches the bowl.
She places a paw over her heart.

25	AIDA
(softly)
The brightest light
still needs someone to shield it
from the wind.

She rises.
Turns toward the clearing where the others are gathering.
She pauses.
Something in the moss catches her eye.
A tiny glimmer. Right at the base of the crystal needle.
Gone almost instantly.
Aida looks at it. Still. Unreadable.
She says nothing. She turns and walks quietly away.

CUT TO:

EXT. GREAT CLEARING – MORNING	3
The clearing is busy with activity.
Everyone is getting ready for the day.
AMIE moves through it all like water.
She helps Sunny untangle her garland.
She finds Misty’s lost crystal pouch.
She steadies Howey’s lopsided flower crown without being asked.
She catches Fuzzby’s honey pot before it rolls off the log.
She is everywhere. She is wonderful.
And nobody notices.
Not because they don’t care.
Just because she makes it look so easy.

KEEN watches her from across the clearing.
Something about it sits with him. He can’t quite name it yet.

LUNA needs someone to help carry the picnic things.

26	LUNA
Amie — would you mind?

Amie is already carrying three things.
She opens her mouth. Closes it. Smiles.

27	AMIE
Of course!

She takes the picnic basket too. Four things now.
Her Amethyst flickers. Just once. Nobody sees.

SUNNY wants someone to help arrange the decorations.

28	SUNNY
Amie you’re so good at this — could you…?

Amie is already carrying four things.
She opens her mouth. Something almost comes out. Almost.
She smiles.

29	AMIE
Sure!

Five things now. Her Amethyst flickers again. Dimmer this time.
Keen sees it. Just for a second. His eyes narrow slightly.

MISTY appears with an armful of crystals that need sorting.

30	MISTY
Amie — I don’t suppose you could…?

HOWEY steps forward. Calm. Steady. Certain.

31	HOWEY
I’ve got it Misty.

Misty blinks. Howey looks at Amie. Just for a moment.
She looks back. She knows he saw.

32	AMIE
(quietly)
Thank you Howey.

33	HOWEY
(equally quiet)
I know.

FUZZBY appears beside Amie.
He is carrying absolutely nothing.
He looks at everything she is carrying.
He reaches out very slowly.
Takes the picnic basket from her.
He holds it at arm’s length. Awkwardly.
Like he has never held a picnic basket before.
Which he hasn’t.

34	FUZZBY
(very serious)
I will carry this.

Amie stares at him.

35	AMIE
Fuzzby you don’t have to—

36	FUZZBY
I will carry this.

He carries it approximately three steps before it is too heavy.
He puts it down on a log. Looks at it.

37	FUZZBY
(to the basket)
Stay.

He flies back to Amie.

38	FUZZBY
(proudly)
I carried it.

Amie looks at the basket three steps away on the log.
Then at Fuzzby. Then back at the basket.

39	AMIE
(gently)
You did Fuzzby.
Thank you.

Fuzzby beams. Zenny drifts past.

40	ZENNY
(deadpan)
Three steps.

41	FUZZBY
Three very good steps.

Amie smiles. A real one.
Her Amethyst flickers warmly for a moment.
Then dims again.

KEEN watches from across the clearing.
He has seen enough.
He looks at AIDA.
She is already looking at him.
She gives the smallest nod.

42	KEEN
(to Amie, quietly)
Has anyone ever told you
it’s okay to put things down?

Amie looks at him.

43	AMIE
(lightly)
I’m fine Keen.

44	KEEN
I know.
That’s not what I asked.

Amie opens her mouth. Closes it.
She doesn’t have an answer.

CUT TO:

EXT. FOREST PATH – AFTERNOON	4
The bears walk together through the forest.
Chattering. Laughing. Easy.
AMIE walks slightly behind the group.
Still carrying more than her share.
She slows.
Something catches her eye.
Off the path. In the roots of an old tree.
A glimmer.
She stops. Looks at it.
It catches the light in a way that seems… wrong.
Too bright. Too still.
She takes one step toward it.
The glimmer vanishes.
Just roots and moss. Nothing there.
Amie stares at the spot.
The group carries on ahead.

45	KEEN
(calling back)
Amie? You okay?

Amie looks at the spot one more time.
Opens her mouth. Almost says something. Almost.
She smiles.

46	AMIE
Fine! Coming!

She catches up with the group.
From the back of the group —
AIDA glances at the same spot in the roots.
Her expression unreadable. She says nothing. She walks on.

CUT TO:

EXT. STREAM – LATE AFTERNOON	5
The stream catches the afternoon light.
Everyone has gone ahead.
AMIE sits alone on a mossy rock beside the water.
Her arms are finally empty.
Her AMETHYST crystal is almost dark.
She looks at it. Then at the water.
She hasn’t sat still all day.
It feels strange. Uncomfortable almost.
Like she doesn’t know what to do with herself
when she isn’t doing something for someone else.

Footsteps on the moss.
HOWEY appears. He sits beside her.
Not too close. Just close enough.
He doesn’t say anything at first.
Just sits.
Amie glances at him.

47	AMIE
(lightly)
You don’t have to sit with me Howey.
Go and enjoy the afternoon.

48	HOWEY
I am enjoying the afternoon.

Beat.

49	AMIE
I’m fine you know.

50	HOWEY
I know.

A beat.

51	HOWEY
I said that a lot too.

Amie looks at him. Something shifts.

52	AMIE
(quietly)
I just… don’t want to be a bother.
Everyone has their own things.
I don’t want to add to it.

53	HOWEY
So instead you carry theirs.

Amie doesn’t answer. She looks at the water.
Which is answer enough.

54	HOWEY
Amie.

55	AMIE
Yes?

56	HOWEY
What do YOU need?

Amie opens her mouth. Closes it.
Nobody has asked her that before.
Not like that. Not waiting for a real answer.
Her eyes glisten.

57	AMIE
(very small)
I don’t know.
I’m not sure I know how to… ask.

58	HOWEY
That is okay.
I did not know how to stop.
You helped me.

He turns to face her properly.

59	HOWEY
I am here now.
You do not have to carry anything.
Not today.

Amie looks at him. A long beat.
Then something releases in her.
Quiet. Real. Like a breath she has been holding all day.

60	AMIE
(barely above a whisper)
I just wanted someone to notice.

61	HOWEY
(simply)
I noticed.

Amie nods. Her eyes close for a moment.
The stream moves gently past.

Footsteps on the moss.
FUZZBY appears.
He is carrying the picnic basket.
All the way from the log.
He is absolutely exhausted.
He sets it down beside Amie with great ceremony.
Collapses dramatically onto a rock.

62	FUZZBY
(gasping)
I brought the basket.

Amie stares at him.

63	AMIE
Fuzzby… that’s very far.

64	FUZZBY
(still recovering)
I am aware.

65	HOWEY
How long did that take you?

66	FUZZBY
A very long time Howey.
A very long time.

He opens the basket.
Produces a small piece of honeycomb.
Holds it out to Amie.
His arm is shaking slightly from the effort.

67	FUZZBY
(quietly, seriously)
This is for you.

Amie looks at the honeycomb.
Then at Fuzzby.
Her eyes fill.

68	AMIE
(softly)
Thank you Fuzzby.

69	FUZZBY
(waving it off)
It is nothing.

Beat.

70	FUZZBY
It is actually quite a lot.
My arms are very tired.

Howey laughs. Amie laughs. Even Fuzzby laughs.
ZENNY appears from behind a leaf.

71	ZENNY
(warmly)
Well done Fuzzby.

72	FUZZBY
(surprised)
Was that… a compliment Zenny?

73	ZENNY
BZZZZZZ.

74	FUZZBY
I will take that as a yes.

CUT TO:

EXT. STREAM – CONTINUOUS	6
The light is golden now. Late and warm.
KEEN has appeared quietly at the edge of the treeline.
He sits down on the bank. Close but not intrusive.

75	KEEN
(gently)
For someone who helps everyone else feel seen…
you’re pretty good at being invisible.

Amie laughs. A real one. Short and surprised.

76	AMIE
Is that a compliment?

77	KEEN
It’s an observation.
From someone who’s been watching.

Amie looks at her Amethyst. Almost dark.
She turns it in her paw.

78	AMIE
(quietly)
I think I forgot…
that I’m allowed to need things too.

Howey places a large steady paw over hers.

79	HOWEY
You are allowed.

Amie takes a breath.
She touches her Amethyst pendant.

80	AMIE
From within I shine so bright —
Amethyst, let us shine the light.

Purple light blooms from the crystal.
Warm. Deep. Rich. Fuller than it has been all day.
Howey touches his Howlite.

81	HOWEY
Steady and bright, with peaceful might —
Howlite, guide us right.

White light joins the purple.
Keen touches his wristbands.

82	KEEN
Brave and bright, with all my might —
Aquamarine, shine with light!

Blue light joins.
Three crystals. Three lights.
Shimmering across the surface of the stream.
The water carries them downstream.
Beautiful.
Amie watches them go.
Her crystal glows full and warm.
Not because everything is fixed.
Because she finally let someone in.

CUT TO:

EXT. GREAT CLEARING – EVENING	7
The clearing is warm and golden.
Fireflies drift between the trees.
The whole group is together.
But something is different tonight.
Nobody is asking Amie for anything.
Nobody is handing her things to carry.
Nobody needs anything from her at all.
They are all just… there.
For her.

LUNA has laid out a soft cushion in the centre of the circle.
Just for Amie.
She didn’t announce it. She just did it.
MISTY has arranged Amie’s favourite flowers around it.
HOWEY has carried the food. All of it. By himself.
He is absolutely fine.
He is not fine.
But he keeps going because it’s for Amie.

Amie arrives and stops.
She looks at the cushion.
At the flowers.
At all of them waiting for her.
She opens her mouth.

83	AMIE
You didn’t have to—

84	LUNA
(firmly, warmly)
Sit down Amie.

Amie sits.
It takes her a moment.
She is not used to being in the middle.
She is always at the edge.
Always just outside the circle.
But here she is.
Right in the centre of it.
And everyone is looking at her.
Not because they need something.
Just because they want to.

LUNA passes her a bowl of her favourite berries without a word.
MISTY puts a warm blanket around her shoulders.

85	MISTY
(quietly)
You’re always looking after everyone else.
Let us look after you tonight.

Amie looks at the blanket. Then at Misty.
Her chin wobbles slightly.

86	AMIE
(very small)
I don’t really know how.

87	MISTY
(smiling)
That’s okay.
We’ll show you.

SUNNY appears with a garland of flowers.
She places it carefully on Amie’s head.
Steps back. Looks at her.

88	SUNNY
You look beautiful.

89	AMIE
(surprised)
Sunny…

90	SUNNY
You do!
You always do!
I just never said it out loud before!
I’m saying it now!

Everyone laughs. Amie laughs too.
A real laugh. Full and warm.
Her Amethyst glows brighter.

HOWEY sits down beside her.
Very close. Like a large warm wall.

91	HOWEY
(quietly)
How are you feeling?

92	AMIE
(honestly)
Like I put something down.
And I didn’t realise how heavy it was.

93	HOWEY
(nodding slowly)
Yes.
That is exactly what it feels like.

A beat.

94	AMIE
Thank you Howey.
For noticing.

95	HOWEY
Always.

FUZZBY appears.
He is wearing his honeycomb lasso around his neck like a medal.
He sits down directly in front of Amie.
Very seriously. He points at the lasso.

96	FUZZBY
(very solemn)
I have decided the lasso was not a failure.
It was a prototype.

97	AMIE
What is the next version?

98	FUZZBY
Longer.

Amie laughs. A real one. Full and warm.
Her Amethyst glows brighter.

99	FUZZBY
(quietly, seriously)
I carried that basket a very long way today Amie.

100	AMIE
I know Fuzzby.

101	FUZZBY
My arms are still tired.

102	AMIE
(warmly)
Thank you Fuzzby.

103	FUZZBY
(waving it off grandly)
It is nothing.

Beat.

104	FUZZBY
It was actually very far.
But for you… worth it.

Amie smiles. Her eyes fill.
Fuzzby nods very seriously. His work here is done.

ZENNY drifts over quietly.
She settles beside Amie.
Says nothing. Just sits.
Which from Zenny is everything.

105	AMIE
(looking at them all)
I didn’t know you all…

She stops. Tries again.

106	AMIE
I didn’t know it was okay to…

She stops again.
Her eyes fill. She looks at the ground.

107	LUNA
(gently)
We know.

A long warm beat.
The fireflies drift between them.
Amie’s Amethyst glows full and warm and steady.
The fullest it has been all day.
All episode.
Maybe ever.

108	AMIE
(quietly, to all of them)
Thank you.
All of you.

Nobody says anything.
They don’t need to.
They just sit together in the golden light.
All of them. Together.

Slowly the group drifts.
Howey walks Luna and Misty home.
Sunny skips after them still wearing flowers.
Fuzzby attempts to lasso a passing firefly.
Misses. Shrugs. Follows the others.

109	FUZZBY
(to the firefly, walking away)
Tomorrow.

Zenny drifts after him.
AMIE watches them all go.
Her crystal warm. Her paws empty. Her heart full.
She takes a long slow breath.
Lets it out.
And for the first time all day…
She is just Amie.
And that is more than enough.
She heads quietly into the trees.

The clearing is almost empty now.
Just KEEN and AIDA.
Sitting together in the fading light.
A comfortable silence.
The kind that only happens between people who trust each other completely.
Keen looks at his wristbands.
Turns them slowly.
He does this sometimes without thinking.
Aida watches him.
Her expression soft. Full of something.
She has been waiting for the right moment for a very long time.
This is it.

110	AIDA
(very gently)
Keen.

111	KEEN
Yeah?

Aida looks at the wristbands. Then at Keen.

112	AIDA
I knew your father.

Keen goes completely still.
The whole world goes still.

113	KEEN
(barely a whisper)
What?

114	AIDA
A long time ago.
Before you were born.
He came to Crystal Cove…
just like you did.

Keen stares at her.
His paws close around the wristbands.

115	KEEN
The wristbands…

116	AIDA
(softly)
I gave them to him.
Many moons ago.
He wore them every day.

A long beat.
Keen looks down at his wrists.

And then — very softly — as if carried on the breeze —

His MUM’S VOICE. Warm. Distant. Like a memory surfacing.

117	MUM
(whisper)
Promise me you’ll wear them.

A flash of the pier.
Keen as a younger bear.
His Mum kneeling in front of him.
Her eyes full of something she doesn’t say.
She places the wristbands carefully in his paws.
One. Then the other.
Her paws wrapped around his.
Holding on.

Back to the clearing.

Keen staring at his wrists.
At the wristbands he has worn since that day.
Since he promised.
Everything he thought he knew about them
shifts quietly into something bigger.
Something that was always there.
Waiting for him to be ready to understand it.

118	KEEN
(voice breaking slightly)
He was here?

119	AIDA
He was brave.
Just like you.
A brave bear.

Keen looks down at his wristbands.
A long beat.
The fireflies drift through the clearing.
The wristbands glow very softly.
Azure blue. Warm and steady.

CUT TO:

EXT. BUZZING NOOK – NIGHT	8
Moonlight. Silence.
The honeycomb hangs from its branch.
Glistening. Perfect. Untouched.
A bush nearby rustles.
Very slowly.
A pair of eyes appears through the leaves.
Then a nose.
Then KEEN.
He is wearing leaves on his head.
And sticks on his arms.
He is attempting to be a bush.
He is not a convincing bush.
He inches forward. One tiny step. Then another.
The honeycomb is right there. So close.
He reaches out one paw.
Almost—

120	FUZZBY
(appearing from nowhere, arms folded)
Never.

Keen freezes. Still in bush position. Leaves on head.

121	KEEN
I’m a bush.

122	FUZZBY
You are not a bush.

123	KEEN
I could be a bush.

124	FUZZBY
Bushes do not have tails.

Keen looks at his tail. It twitches.

125	KEEN
(to his tail)
Not helping.

126	FUZZBY
Never. Ever. Ever. Ever.

Beat.

127	KEEN
You said ever four times.

128	FUZZBY
I meant it four times.

Keen sighs.
Removes the leaves from his head.
Drops the sticks.
Walks away with what little dignity remains.
Which is not much.

Fuzzby watches him go. Very satisfied.
He turns back to his honeycomb. His. Safe.
He notices KEEN’S honeycomb sitting on a log nearby.
Unattended. Gleaming.
His eyes go very wide.
He looks left. He looks right.
He reaches into his sack.
Pulls out a tiny pair of makeshift wings.
Made of leaves.
He covers them in old honey from his sack.
Straps them carefully to his back. On top of his actual wings.
He is disguising himself as a different bee.
This is his plan.
He tiptoes forward. Arms out.
Buzzing very quietly to himself.
Like a different bee would.
Normal bee sounds. Nothing suspicious.
He reaches the log. The honeycomb is right there.
He reaches out—

129	KEEN
(without turning around)
Never. Ever. Fuzzby.

Fuzzby freezes. Leaf wings at full extension.
One foot in the air. A very very long beat.

He tries to remove the leaf wings.
They don’t come off.
He pulls harder.
Still stuck.
The honey has hardened.
He is now permanently a different bee.
He stares at his wings.

130	FUZZBY
(quietly)
I used old honey.

131	KEEN
(trying not to laugh)
How much old honey?

132	FUZZBY
A lot of old honey.

ZENNY is nearby.
She looks at Fuzzby.
At the leaf wings.
At the honey.
She loses it completely.
Properly. Fully. No composure whatsoever.

133	FUZZBY
(outraged)
Don’t you BZZZZZZ me Zenny.

134	ZENNY
(crying with laughter)
BZZZZZZ.

135	FUZZBY
(genuinely baffled)
I was a different bee.

136	KEEN
You were not a different bee.

137	FUZZBY
I had different wings.

138	KEEN
They were leaves Fuzzby.

139	FUZZBY
Very convincing leaves.

Beat.

140	KEEN
How did you even strap them on.

141	FUZZBY
I have been planning this all day.

Keen turns around.
Looks at Fuzzby. The leaf wings. The tiny sack. The absolute confidence.
He stares at him for a very long time.

142	KEEN
(quietly)
You are the most incredible creature I have ever met.

143	FUZZBY
(straightening up proudly)
I know.

They sit down side by side beneath the honeycomb.
Neither of them moving.
Both of them looking up at it.
Moonlight. Silence. The honeycomb glowing just out of reach.
A comfortable beat.

144	FUZZBY
Tomorrow.

145	KEEN
Tomorrow.

146	ZENNY
Fuzzby you just won’t give up will you.

147	FUZZBY
Never. Ever.

He turns back to the wings.
Pulls again.
Still stuck.
Keen and Zenny look at each other.
They both start laughing.
Fuzzby looks at them.
Then at his wings.
Then back at them.

148	FUZZBY
(very seriously)
I am in a sticky situation.

The forest is quiet.
Everything is exactly as it should be.

FADE OUT.

THE END
