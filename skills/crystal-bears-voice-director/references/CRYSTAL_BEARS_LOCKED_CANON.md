## 1. Show Canon

- **Title:** The Crystal Bears
- **Format:** CGI — 26 × 11′ episodes + 26 × 2′ digital shorts (plus daily/bedtime/funtime/stack short-form formats)
- **Target:** Ages 4–8, co-viewing (parent leans in)
- **Look:** Pixar/DreamWorks-grade 3D CGI — soft plush fur, subsurface scattering, large expressive eyes, soft global illumination, cinematic depth of field. NEVER 2D, hand-drawn, or flat.
- **Premise:** Seven crystal-powered bears help kids turn big feelings into strengths, in Crystal Cove — a world where emotions shape reality. Crystals don't grant powers; they *amplify a strength the bear already carries.*
- **Story engine:** The Five Emotional Pillars (§2).
- **Tagline:** *Kindness is a power-up.* / *The emotion is the mechanic. The entertainment is the engine. The kindness is the power-up.*
- **No villains.** The antagonist is always the *feeling itself*, never a character.

## 7. Directing Profile — Ages 4–8 Pixar (the "Crystal Bears lens")

The directing rules that make this audience-grade (enforced by the future Director skill):
- **Hold shots longer; cut less.** No rapid montage. Strong establishing shot on every scene change — kids must always know *where* they are.
- **Camera honest & legible.** Eye-level or gentle low angle. No extreme dutch/whip-pan/showy moves.
- **One clear focal point per shot;** readable silhouettes; character-centred staging.
- **Emotions big and clear** — cut to a close-up on every emotional beat; the face is the story.
- **High-key, warm, saturated, soft** lighting; rounded huggable forms.
- **Gentle jeopardy that always resolves;** no horror framing; warmth is the baseline.
- **One clean action per shot;** strong anticipation; visible cause-and-effect (12 principles enforced).
- **Show the feeling, never name it.** No teaching voice, no "remember kids…". The parent gets a mirror, not a wink.
- These choices propagate to post: simpler/slower dialogue, warm motif-driven score with mild dynamics, gentle sound design.

## 8. Voice Generation Standard (ElevenLabs V3) — NON-NEGOTIABLE

ALL Crystal Bears dialogue is generated in **ElevenLabs V3** with **audio tags** — the bracketed emotion/acting cues are what carry the cadence, the performance, and the character. Plain un-tagged text loses the acting and is **forbidden**. The authoritative engine is the **`crystal-bears-voice`** skill — the full tag bible, per-character settings, and direction craft live there; the app calls that logic rather than duplicating it.

Hard rules the app's audio provider MUST enforce on every call:

1. **Model:** `model_id="eleven_v3"` always — the API defaults to v2, which loses the acting. Set it explicitly.
2. **Audio tags in `[square brackets]`** carry emotion + acting + non-verbals — e.g. `[whispers]`, `[cries]`, `[nervous]`, `[excited]`, `[laughs]`, `[sighs]`, `[breathes]`, `[gulps]`. Tags go *before* the text they affect and persist until the next tag overrides.
3. **1–2 tags per segment.** 3+ tags confuse v3 and flatten the read. The tag sets the colour; the **text does the acting.**
4. **Text formatting does the rhythm:** CAPS = stress (1–2 words/line), ellipsis = hesitation, em-dash = interruption/breath-catch, commas = slowed delivery, periods = weight.
5. **No SSML `<break>`** — use `[pause]` / `[long pause]`.
6. **Per-character settings** (Stability / Similarity / Staggeration from `crystal-bears-voice`) + the locked **voice IDs (§5)** applied on every call. Lower stability (≈25–50) for expressive cartoon delivery; **stability above ~40 stops tags working** on character voices.
7. **Crystal Calls:** generated ALONE, Stability 45 / Staggeration 15, minimum 3 takes (v3 is non-deterministic — multi-take every emotional peak).
8. **Voices:** Instant Voice Clones (IVC) or designed library voices only (PVCs aren't fully tag-responsive). Never set `use_speaker_boost` (unavailable in v3).

**Pipeline implication — dialogue & lip-sync.** Dialogue **is spoken in the video** so the mouth lip-syncs to real speech — but the model's voice is a **throwaway source** and never ships. In Post the final voice is always the canonical **ElevenLabs** voice, by one of two paths: **(default — video is the timing master)** strip the native audio from the clip and run it through **ElevenLabs Voice Changer (speech-to-speech)** to swap the vocal identity while keeping the exact timing/phrasing — **never re-generate with text-to-speech (fresh timing drifts off the lips → the gap)**; **(hero / Heart lines — audio is the timing master, to keep the V3 bracket-acted performance)** generate the **V3 acted master first** → lip-sync the video's mouth to it (e.g. HeyGen Avatar IV). The i2v dialogue line must match the script word-for-word. Music is always the Suno track in Post, never from the video.

## Start + END keyframe on EVERY shot (new standard, 2026-06-19, Julian)

Every shot gets a **START keyframe and an END keyframe** (not just action shots). Doubles the (cheap, ~$0.04) keyframes but gives exact control of each shot's full motion arc — we define precisely where the shot begins and ends, prompt it properly, and Seedance animates the true motion between (`image_url` + `end_image_url`). For a near-static hold the two can be nearly identical, but always produce both. Naming: start = `Ep{n}_{code}_{slug}.png` (primary, shown in studio), end = `Ep{n}_{code}_{slug}_end.png`.
